Symptom

During the Executive Review stage, fields remain non-editable despite edit permissions being granted.

Environment

SAP SuccessFactors Compensation

Reproducing the Issue

  1. Navigate to Compensation in SAP SuccessFactors.
  2. Select the affected template.
  3. Click on Executive Review.
  4. Observe that the fields are non-editable despite edit permissions being granted.

Cause

  • Once compensation values have been published to Employee Central (EC) from a form, the system does not allow those form fields to be edited even if the form is moved back to the In-Progress stage. This behavior is expected in SAP SuccessFactors Compensation.

  • This behavior can be observed in the following scenarios:

    1. A form was launched, published, deleted, and then a new form was launched.

    2. A form was launched, moved to the completed step, and later routed back to the in-progress stage.

    3. A user was part of a completed form that was published and then transitioned to another form that is currently in progress.

Resolution

  1. Delete the published compensation records from Employee Central for the affected employees.
  2. Choose one of the following options based on preference:
  • **Option 1 – Edit the form in its current In-Progress state:**

a. Once the published records are removed, the fields in the In-Progress form will become editable again.

  • **Option 2 – Edit without re-routing (keep forms at Completed stage):**

a. Delete the published compensation records from Employee Central for the affected employees.

b. Enable the setting "Allow edit of completed Compensation forms" in the template's Advanced Settings:

i. Navigate to Admin Center → Compensation → Compensation Home → Select the Template → Plan Setup → Settings → Advanced Settings.

ii. Enable/check the "Allow edit of completed Compensation forms" option.

iii. Click the "Update Form Template" button at the bottom of the page to save changes.

c. This will allow editing of fields directly from the Completed stage without needing to re-route.
