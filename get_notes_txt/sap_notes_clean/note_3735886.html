Symptom

You are processing an Accounts Receivable (FI-AR) scenario for Argentina where an invoice was issued in a foreign currency (e.g., USD), but the payment was received in the local currency (ARS). Between the time of invoicing and payment, the exchange rate has changed significantly.

You are unsure of the correct procedure and have the following questions:

  • When posting the incoming payment, which ARS amount should be entered?
  • How should the large difference in ARS between the invoice date and payment date be handled?
  • What is the correct purpose and usage of the Argentina-specific report RFIDAREXRATEDIFF in this process?

Environment

  • SAP S/4HANA
  • SAP S/4HANA Cloud Private Edition
  • SAP ERP
  • Financial Accounting (FI)
  • Accounts Receivable (FI-AR)
  • Accounts Payable (FI-AP)
  • Localization for Argentina Financials (FI-LOC-FI-AR)

Cause

The confusion often arises from a misunderstanding of how four separate processes interact:

  1. Standard SAP Behavior: The system is designed to calculate and post any exchange rate difference to a designated gain or loss account upon final clearing of an open item.
  2. Payment Difference Tolerances: The system’s action—whether it fully clears an invoice with a difference or treats it as a partial payment—depends entirely on the tolerance limits configured for payment differences.
  3. Business Policy: How the principal amount of a realized exchange rate difference is handled is a business decision. Whether your company absorbs the gain/loss or passes it to the customer is a manual policy.
  4. Argentina Localization Requirement: The RFIDAREXRATEDIFF report is a specific tool that addresses only the tax portion of a realized exchange rate difference. It generates a Debit Memo for the tax on a gain and a Credit Memo for the tax on a loss.

Resolution

The process depends on your system’s tolerance settings. We will use one simplified example to explain both potential system behaviors.

  • Invoice: 1,000 USD
  • Exchange Rate at Invoice: 1,000 ARS / USD (Original value is 1,000,000 ARS)
  • Exchange Rate at Payment: 1,200 ARS / USD (Current value is 1,200,000 ARS)
  • Payment Received: 1,000,000 ARS
  • Payment Shortfall: 200,000 ARS

Step 1: Understand the System Behavior on Payment Posting

You must always post the actual amount received (1,000,000 ARS). The system’s reaction to the 200,000 ARS shortfall depends on your tolerance configuration.

Behavior A: Partial Payment (Difference EXCEEDS Tolerance)
This is the standard SAP behavior for large differences and is the most likely outcome.

  • System Action: The system determines the payment is insufficient to clear the full invoice at the current rate and is outside the allowed tolerance. It processes the payment as a partial payment.
  • System Posting:
    1. It calculates how much USD the payment covers: 1,000,000 ARS ÷ 1,200 ARS/USD = 833.33 USD.
    2. It calculates the original value of this cleared portion: 833.33 USD * 1,000 ARS/USD = 833,333.33 ARS.
    3. It compares the payment to the original value of the cleared portion and posts a realized exchange rate gain of 166,666.67 ARS (1,000,000 - 833,333.33).
    4. The invoice remains open with a remaining balance of 166.67 USD.

Behavior B: Full Clearing with Difference (Difference is WITHIN Tolerance)
This happens only if the difference (200,000 ARS) is within the permitted tolerance limits.

  • System Action: The system is authorized to clear the invoice and automatically handle the difference.
  • System Posting: The 1,000,000 ARS payment is posted. The system clears the full 1,000 USD invoice and automatically posts the 200,000 ARS shortfall to the Exchange Rate Loss account.

Step 2: Decide How to Handle the Principal Difference (Business Policy)

This step is only relevant after an exchange rate difference has been posted (as in Behavior B, or after the final clearing payment in Behavior A).

  • Option A: Absorb the Loss (Most Common): No further action is required. The loss is correctly reflected in your P&L statement.
  • Option B: Charge the Customer for the Loss: This is a manual business process. You must manually create a new Debit Memo to charge the customer for the exchange rate loss amount (200,000 ARS).

Step 3: Run the Argentina-Specific Tax Adjustment Report

This is a mandatory compliance step that you run after an exchange rate difference has been posted.

  • Action: Execute the RFIDAREXRATEDIFF report.
  • System Behavior: The report finds the realized exchange rate difference posting. Based on Argentinian tax regulations:
    • For a loss, the report generates a Credit Memo to adjust the taxes for your customer.
    • For a gain, the report generates a Debit Memo to collect the additional tax due from the customer.
