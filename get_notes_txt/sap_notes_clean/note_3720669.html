Symptom

  • This FAQ provides guidance on software updates, maintenance activities, notifications, delayed updates, and general operational behavior in SAP Cloud Integration and SAP Integration Suite.
  • Customers frequently receive various notifications and communications related to planned or ongoing software update activities.

This FAQ consolidates the most common questions to clarify:

  • Understanding SAP notifications
  • Describes what customers should expect during maintenance.
  • Outlines how delayed updates work.
  • Shows how to check the status of updates.
  • Explains how release notes and update history are managed.

Resolution

Communication & Notifications

  1. Will there be a communication from SAP after the software update is complete?
    A: For Cloud Foundry (CF), customers will receive a confirmation communication from SAP once the software update has been successfully completed.
    For Neo, communication is sent only before the update.
  2. Why wasn’t I notified about the failures or software update failure?
    A: Customer communication is sent only after a successful software update. The update was performed with zero downtime. Any internal handling related to the update process is not part of the customer communication flow.
  3. Why don’t customers receive communication when a software update fails?
    A: Notifications are sent only after successful software updates. Updates are executed using a zero-downtime (ZDM) approach, ensuring uninterrupted service for customers.
  4. We have received an email regarding an upcoming software update in CPI. Do we need to take any actions?
    A: In most cases, no action is required from the customer. If any action is needed, SAP will send a dedicated notification with clear instructions and sufficient lead time. The notification will also describe the criteria to help determine whether the action applies to your environment.
  5. Regular software updates do not follow the same schedule as Major Upgrades. Where can I find more information about software updates?
    A: Regular software updates, Major Upgrades, and network-related changes are separate processes and follow different timelines. For further details, see: · Software Update | SAP Help Portal · SAP Cloud Platform Integration : how we do softwar... - SAP Community
  6. Why am I not notified XXX days before the update?
    A: The update is non-disruptive and does not require any action from customers.
  7. How can I be notified in advance about planned updates?
    A: You can stay informed about planned updates by checking the release note available here: https://me.sap.com/notes/3418154
  8. Who should subscribe to the automated emails?
    A: It is up to each customer to decide. At a minimum, the system administrator(s) should subscribe. Business leaders are also advised to stay informed of major changes to ensure proper planning and testing.

Delayed Software Update and Timeslots

  1. How do delayed software updates work?
    A: Delayed updates allow tenants to postpone the scheduled update by one week. This option is useful for customers who want extra time to prepare.
  2. Why are some patches applied midweek instead of during the delayed update slot?
    A: Critical patches or urgent fixes may need to be applied immediately to prevent service disruption. In such cases, the update takes priority over the normal delayed update schedule.
  3.  Can production & non-production environments be updated on different dates?
    A: Customers shall be able to configure delayed updates and define custom update times for their productive and test tenants. Refer to- https://help.sap.com/docs/integration-suite/sap-integration-suite/delay-software-update
  4. What is the Delayed Update mechanism and when is it valid?
    A: Active Scope: Delayed Update is currently available only for monthly feature releases, not for hotfixes or infrastructure-level updates.
    Usage: It allows delaying the deployment to production tenants by one week for testing purposes.
  5. Can a major platform upgrade be delayed for testing in non-production?
    A: No, platform-level upgrades apply to all tenants simultaneously and cannot be postponed individually.
  6. Is it possible to know which new features or patch updates/bug fixes are included in software updates?
    A: Yes. Information about new features and updates is available at the following links:
    - https://help.sap.com/docs/cloud-integration/sap-cloud-integration/what-s-new-for-cloud-integration
    - https://help.sap.com/docs/cloud-integration/sap-cloud-integration/patch-releases-for-cloud-integration
    This information is also included in the communication sent by SAP.
  7. Is there a maintenance schedule for regular Cloud Integration software updates?
    A: Yes. Please refer to SAP Knowledge Base Article [2598838](https://me.sap.com/notes/2598838) for detailed information about the maintenance schedule of SAP Cloud Integration.
  8. Why are communications sent only before Neo updates and after CF updates?
    A: CF updates are performed with 0 downtime in a rolling manner. Since these updates usually involve minimal downtime or disruption, communication is sent after the update to confirm completion.

Software Update Behavior & Tenant Operations

  1. What is the impact of the ongoing update on our tenant?
    A: The update is performed in a rolling manner and with near 0 downtime.
  2. How can I verify if the software update has been completed?
    A: You can check the version of your tenant before the update. To do this, click on your profile in the top-right corner and select ‘About’. Once the update is completed, the version number displayed there will be incremented.
  3. What caused the tenant restart?
    A: The restart was triggered by an operational activity. While update is typically limited to agreed maintenance windows, unplanned restarts e.g. OOM, cell evacuation may occasionally occur without prior notification.
  4. Is there any downtime during the restart?
    A: No downtime occurs for running integrations, as the restart is performed using a rolling restart approach.

Regular Software Updates vs. Major Upgrade

  1. Are Major Upgrades and regular software updates the same?
    A: No. Major upgrades and regular software updates are distinct activities, each with its own schedule, scope, and execution plan. All customer communications will clearly specify the type of activity taking place. Regular software updates are non-disruptive and include only changes related to the Integration Suite. Major upgrades (MUW) may introduce disruptive changes that affect either the Integration Suite or the underlying SAP BTP platform.
    A: Regular software updates, Major Upgrades, and network-related changes are separate processes and follow different timelines.
    For further details, see:
    - https://help.sap.com/docs/cloud-integration/sap-cloud-integration/software-update?q=software+update+zero+downtime
    - https://community.sap.com/t5/technology-blog-posts-by-sap/sap-cloud-platform-integration-how-we-do-software-updates/ba-p/13357939
  2. I have configured delayed software updates. Does this impact the MUW?
    A: No. The delayed software update option applies only to regular software updates. Major upgrades are executed according to the announced schedule, regardless of this setting.
  3. Where can I find information on the harmonized release schedule for SAP Integration Suite and Cloud Integration products?
    A: Customers are notified in advance and should prepare for any impactful changes. This applies to both Cloud Integration and SAP Integration Suite.
    Regular software updates occur on harmonized weekends. They may involve short or no-impact feature deliveries. Customers typically do not need to act, as most changes are transparent. Refer to Maintenance Windows and Major Upgrade Windows for SAP Cloud Services.
  4. Are Major Upgrades and regular software updates performed at the same time?
    A: No. While regular software updates may be planned for the same weekend as a Major Upgrade (MUW), they are always scheduled to start outside of the predefined MUW time slots.

Release Notes

  1. Why aren’t release notes always up to date?
    A: Release notes are tied to the official release decision. Different components may be finalized at different times, so notes may lag the actual update.
  2. How can customers access the most current information?
    A: Customers should refer to the published release notes and any associated official documentation. For urgent or detailed updates, reaching out to support is recommended.

Update Timeslots

  1. Can we get a fixed schedule for updates?
    A: Updates generally follow a scheduled batch process, but exact timing per tenant isn’t fixed. Productive tenants have the option to delay the update by one week, which provides some flexibility.
  2. Can customers choose a specific day for their update?
    A: Customers cannot select a specific day. Updates happen according to the scheduled iteration. For monthly updates, the delayed update option allows shifting the update by a week if needed.

Update History

  1. Can customers access a detailed history of updates?
    A: A detailed update history is not generally available to customers. It is provided only in cases of significant business impact. Standard practice is to notify customers after each update is applied.
  2. What details are included in the history when provided?
    A: This update history can be provided only upon request when a ticket is opened under the “LOD-HCI-PI-OPS” component. The history typically includes details such as
    - Update version and type
    - Date and time applied
