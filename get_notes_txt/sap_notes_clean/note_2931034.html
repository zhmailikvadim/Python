  • Want to mass update the S-user lifetime but the Expiry Date is grayed out

Environment

SAP for Me > User Management

Resolution

Only Super/Cloud/User Administrators can change the S-user Expiration (Expiry) Date of S-user IDs in the User Management application.

  • You can mass extend the Expiry Date of a group of S-users that have a status of Active*, Expiring, or Expired from 1 day to 24 months.
  • The mass update of the Expiry Date of S-users can be set to a maximum of 24 months. If you want to specify longer expiry date (up to 60 months) edit the expiry date of each S-user ID individually (manually) as described in KBA 2930885.
  • You can mass extend the Expiry date of a maximum of 500 S-users.

*An active user is a user who has logged in within the last 12 months or was created within the last 12 months.

These steps describe how to mass extend the Expiry Date of S-users with a Status of Active, Expiring, or Expired (minimum: 1 day, maximum: 24 months).

  1. Logon User Management 
  2. On the Users tab, select the group of S-users (note: you cannot select User, Cloud, or Super Admins that with expiry date 31.12.9999, otherwise the Change Expiry Date button will be grayed out)
  3. Click Change Expiry Date at the top of the user list



  4. Select new Expiry Date
  5. Click Save
  • You will see a confirmation: The expiry date for the S-users has been changed successfully.
  • The S-users will now have Status Change Requested (due to a request, a change of the user's expiry date is already being processed)
  • After a few minutes the status of the S-users will change to Active and the new Expiry Date will be visible.
