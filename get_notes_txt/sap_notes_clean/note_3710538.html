General Questions

  • Where can I find the statutory regulation for the active pension?
    Answer: The regulations relevant for payroll for the active pension can be found in Sec. 3 No. 21 EStG (prerequisites, tax exemption, and so on), Sec. 41 Para. 1 Clause 4 EStG (Payroll Account), Sec. 41b Para. 1 Clause 2 No. 5 EStG (Employment Tax Statement), Sec. 42b Para. 1 Clause 3 No. 4 EStG (No Annual Employment Tax Declaration), and Sec. 1 Para. 1 Para. 1 Contrib. 1 Clause 1 (Contrib. 1 Clause 1) 

  • Where can I find more information about the active pension?
    Answer: The BMF has provided a list of questions and answers (FAQ) for the active pension (link).

  • What are the prerequisites for using the asset-side annuity?
    Answer: The following prerequisites must be met for the application of the asset-side annuity:
    • Active Employment
    • The following month after reaching the standard retirement age. If the RAG is reached on the first of the month, this condition is already fulfilled for this month.
    • Work relationship subject to PI (employer pays contributions according to Sec. 168 Para. 1 No. 1 or No. 1d or Para. 3, Sec. 172 Para. 1 or Sec. 172a SGB VI)
    • Tax class 1 – 5 or Tax class 6 with proof from the employee that he or she does not use the active pension in any other secondary employment relationship. 

  • Does the employer have to apply the active pension or can the employee declare a waiver?
    Answer: The employer must apply this if the prerequisites for the active pension for an employee are met. See also FAQ BMF I. No. 3 (link):
    "Is the employer obliged to take the active pension into account for the employment tax deduction? Yes. The employer is obliged to make the employment tax deduction in accordance with the legal requirements. This also includes the consideration of the exemption amount from the active pension in the employment tax deduction procedure."

Tax Exemption Questions

  • What is the maximum tax exemption due to the capitalized pension?
    Answer: The law allows up to EUR 24,000 tax-exempt annually if the prerequisites for the asset pension are met throughout the year. Monthly, a maximum of EUR 2,000 is tax-exempt.

  • In which sequence does the tax exemption take place for the regular and other gross tax amount?
    Answer: First, the regular gross tax amount is reduced. If the maximum tax-exempt available amount (EUR 2,000) is not fully utilized, the other gross tax amount is reduced.

  • Supplementary Pension Public Sector (SPF) - How does the active pension affect the calculation of the contribution?
    Answer: In the current delivery, the tax exemption due to the active pension has no influence on the remuneration subject to SP. The point is currently in clarification.

  • Employee Association Contributions Bremen and Saarland - How does the active pension affect the calculation of the contribution?
    Answer:
    • Employee Association Bremen - Assessment basis for calculating the contributions is the taxable wage. As a result of the tax exemption of the active pension, the assessment basis for the Chamber of Labor contribution is also reduced.
    • Employee Association Saarland - Assessment basis for calculating the contributions is the gross remuneration that is subject to social insurance liability. As a result, the active pension has no effect on the calculation of the Chamber of Labor contribution for Saarland.
       
  • Construction Industry - How does the active pension affect the calculation of contributions?
    Answer: The regulations for the capitalized pension have no effect on the contribution obligation, contribution calculation, or the notification for SOKA-BAU. For industrial active pensioners, the contribution is measured unchanged in the gross wage. For salaried active pensioners, the contribution obligation is based on the respective employment relationship.

Questions about delivery, setup, and functionality

  • Which SAP Notes deliver the function?
    Answer: The initial provision of the functionality is provided with the following SAP Notes:
    3669507 - Asset-side pension: Delivery of function
    3711333 - Active Pension: Remuneration Statement
    3711362 - Asset-side pension: Payroll account
    3704500 - Active pension: Display of tax exemption in employment tax statement for 2026
    After the delivery, refer to SAP Notes that may still be released with adjustments and corrections.

  • Which setup steps are required to use the "Asset Pension" function in the system?
    Answer: For the steps for setting up the function, see SAP Note 3669507 - Active annuity: Delivery of the function. When you import and transport the function to the production system, it is activated automatically for cases that meet the prerequisites for the active pension.
    For customer-specific special features, the check for the prerequisites for the active pension (payroll function DST ARIS) can be adjusted using the implementation of the BAdI HRPAYDE_B_AR_CHECK. (Implementation Guide: Payroll: Germany -> Tax -> Processing -> Active Annuity -> BAdI: Check Prerequisites for Active Pension  or Before HRSP Import Using Transaction SE18)

  • Where can I find additional SAP-specific information about the function and examples for the tax exemption?
    Answer: An overview or You can find the initial screen for the SAP functions in the Implementation Guide: Payroll: Germany -> Tax -> Processing -> Active Pension -> Active Pension - Overview (or, before HRSP implementation, you can use the F1 help to find one of the fields for the active pension in the infotype Tax Data D (0012) and the item More Information on the Active Pension here).

  • Remuneration statement: Where can I find the information for including the active pension in the remuneration statement?
    Answer: The steps for including the active pension in the remuneration statement are described in SAP Note 3711333 - Active pension: Remuneration statement.

  • Payroll account: Where can I find the information for including the active pension in the payroll account?
    Answer: The steps for including the active pension in the payroll account are described in SAP Note 3711362 - Active pension: Payroll account.

  • Company pensions - How are company pensioners recognized and excluded from the active pension?
    Answer: In the conversion using the PI indicator (RVKZ1), no insurance liability is recognized in the infotype Social Insurance D (0013) and excluded from the active pension. 

Questions about the employment tax statement

  • How is the capitalized pension displayed for the year 2026?
    Answer: The statement for the year 2026 is made using a (customer-specific) additional line with the description 'Tax-Exempt Amount Active Annuity'. For more information, see SAP Note 3704500 - Active pension: Display of tax exemption in the employment tax statement for 2026.

  • Does the additional line have to be included in the form for 2026?
    Answer: Yes, only by including the additional line with the description 'Tax-Exempt Amount Active Pension' is it ensured that the tax-exempt amounts are also reported to the tax authority.

  • What is the planning for 2027?
    Answer: It is planned that the asset pension will be included in the form published by the authority.

  • How does the active pension affect the display of social insurance contributions on the ETStmt? 
    Answer: The social insurance contributions are to be displayed proportionally tax-exempt/subject to tax on the employment tax statement. Due to the tax exemption due to the active pension, the social insurance contributions displayed are reduced or no social insurance contribution is displayed.
    Example: Due to the asset pension, the annual gross tax amount is reduced from EUR 48,000 to EUR 24,000. On the employment tax statement, only the social insurance contributions due for the remaining gross tax amount are displayed proportionally. In this case, therefore, half of the total contributions.
