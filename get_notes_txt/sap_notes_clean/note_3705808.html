Symptom

  • When can I request a formal Root Cause Analysis (RCA)
  • How do I request an RCA?

Resolution

RCAs can be requested directly by the customer only for production system issues that meet the criteria:

  • An unplanned system outage resulting in complete service unavailability for a duration exceeding 10 minutes.
  • A global service disruption with a duration exceeding 15 minutes.
  • Persistent performance degradation that materially impacts service usability for a period exceeding 1 hour.
  • Critical access failures, including but not limited to users being unable to authenticate, maintain active sessions, or experiencing unexpected session termination.
  • A failure of a critical application workflow for which no workaround exists, rendering the affected functionality unusable. Example: complete failure of the reporting functionality (excluding isolated report-level issues).
  • An application workflow failure resulting in significant business disruption.

If the formal criteria is met, request a formal RCA via an SAP Support Case:

  1. Update the original case to SAP Fieldglass Support where the issue was reported. Request an official RCA.
  2. If the original case is closed, create a new case.
    • In the Description: Provide the original case details and original Case Number.
  3. After SAP Fieldglass Support validates the request according to the RCA criteria, they will transfer the request to SAP Fieldglass Product Engineering
    • Note. You will get a new case where you can communicate directly with SAP Product Engineering and Support will close their case with you.
  4. Respond to the new case with Product Engineering to request any updates on the RCA. 

Note: RCA process updates are as of January 2026
