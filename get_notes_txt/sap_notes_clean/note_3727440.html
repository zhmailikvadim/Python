Symptom

In Advanced Intercompany Sales with Transportation Management (TM) integration, the Internal Transfer of Control (ToC) date is determined from the planned arrival at Incoterm Location 2, even though the expectation is that the system should use Incoterm Location 1.

In an Advanced Intercompany Sales scenario, transportation planning is performed in Transportation Management and freight units contain multiple stops representing different transportation stages.

The business document contains two Incoterm locations:

  • Incoterm Location 1 – typically representing the shipping or origin location

  • Incoterm Location 2 – typically representing the destination location

Example:

LocationPlanned ArrivalExpected for ToCActual
Port 1 (Incoterm Location 2)<Planned arrival date/time>NoUsed
Port 2 (Incoterm Location 1)<Planned arrival date/time>ExpectedNot used

In this scenario, the business expects the planned arrival at Port 2 (Incoterm Location 1) to determine the Internal Transfer of Control date.

However, the system determines the Transfer of Control based on the planned arrival at Port 1 (Incoterm Location 2).

Environment

SAP S/4HANA 2022 and Higher

Reproducing the Issue

  1. Open the Transportation Cockpit.
  2. Load the delivery using the appropriate selection profile.
  3. Select all entries in the Transportation Cockpit.
  4. Execute Optimizer Planning to create the Freight Order.
  5. Check the internal transfer of control dates.

Cause

  • This behavior is standard system design.
  • For C-Incoterms (CFR, CIF, CPT, CIP), the transfer of risk occurs at the destination location, which corresponds to Incoterm Location 2.
  • By design, incoterm location 2 will not get a stage on the Freight Unit (FU). It is presented on the FU general data tab and used later in follow-on processes, such as capacity document creation, but it is not represented as a separate stage on the FU.
  • The system derives the planned internal transfer of control date exclusively from Transportation Management–relevant milestones generated on Freight Unit stages.

Resolution

For C-Incoterms, the Transfer of Control date is determined based on the planned arrival at Incoterm Location 2, because this location represents the risk transfer point defined by the Incoterm.

If the Internal Transfer of Control should instead occur at the origin location (Incoterm Location 1) or another point in the transportation route, one of the following adjustments may be required:

Option 1 : Use an Incoterm where the risk transfer occurs at the desired location, (or)
Option 2 : Adjust the Incoterm locations maintained in the relevant business documents or master data so that the intended transfer location corresponds to Incoterm Location 2.
