Symptom

This KBA aims to have an explanation about how the HCM solution act to IMSS (Instituto Mexicano do Seguro Social) obligations.

Environment

  • HR renewal 

  • SAP S/4HANA 

  • SAP ERP  

  • SAP SuccessFactors Employee Central Payroll 

Resolution

Two Main Elements of SDI

A. Fixed Elements

Fixed elements are contractual benefits that employees receive consistently and predictably. These amounts are guaranteed by the employment contract and must be integrated into the SDI from the first day the employee is entitled to receive them.

Characteristics:

  • Concepts or benefits guaranteed by employment contract
  • Employee always receives these amounts
  • Fixed and predictable
  • IMSS requires integration from day one of entitlement

Integration Requirements:

  • Must be integrated from the first day of entitlement
  • Requires immediate recalculation when the fixed benefit amount changes (new SDI)
  • Affects social security contributions going forward

Classic Example - Food Vouchers (Vale de Despensa): Some companies provide fixed food voucher amounts regardless of:

  • Employee absences
  • Disabilities
  • Vacations
B. Variable Elements

Variable elements are benefits where the amount is uncertain and cannot be predetermined with certainty. These are accumulated and calculated at the end of each bimonthly period to determine a new SDI.

Characteristics:

  • Uncertain amount and timing of payment
  • Cannot be predetermined with certainty
  • Accumulated at end of bimonthly period
  • Absences and disabilities are subtracted from the calculation

Common Examples:

  • Triple overtime pay (horas extras)
  • Sunday premiums (primas dominicales)
  • Bonuses
  • Awards
  • Commissions
  • Other variable payments

Formula:

Variable SDI = (Sum of Variable Payments in Bimonthly Period) ÷ (Days Present - Absences - Disabilities)
