Symptom

Buyers may receive email notifications stating that SAP Fieldglass will begin enforcing encryption key expiration starting in June 2026.

Buyers may be unclear about:

  • Why they are receiving this notification
  • What actions are required
  • Where to find more information
  • Whether their existing encryption keys meet new security requirements

Environment

Production and Non-production

Reproducing the Issue

Buyers will receive an email notification on this topic. Target audience for change information:

  1. An organization that is currently using:
    • A weak encryption cipher (CAST 128‑bit), or
    • An RSA key size below updated SAP Fieldglass standards for PGP keys
  2. Encryption keys used for integrations are approaching expiration under new enforcement rules
  3. Recipients are listed in the configuration settings managed by the customer’s configuration manager

Cause

SAP Fieldglass is implementing enhanced security controls by enforcing expiration of encryption keys beginning June 2026.

  • These changes were first communicated in the November 2025 release.
  • New automatically generated PGP keys will use:
    • AES‑256 encryption, and
    • 2048‑bit RSA wrapping
  • Customer environments currently using weaker encryption standards will trigger email notifications and in‑application alerts.

PGP keys that are customer‑generated or SAP‑generated both require manual rotation. SAP Fieldglass does not provide automatic rotation for PGP keys.

Resolution

To ensure uninterrupted secure integrations, customers should take the following actions:

1. Review your current encryption key strength

Verify whether your organization is using:

  • CAST 128‑bit encryption (weak and being retired), or
  • An RSA key size below 2048‑bit

If so, rotation is required before enforcement begins in June 2026.

2. Confirm your systems support the new encryption standards

Ensure all internal systems and integrations can process PGP keys encrypted with:

  • AES‑256, and
  • 2048‑bit RSA

3. If your systems cannot support the new standards

You must generate and provide your own PGP key pair that meets the following requirements:

  • Contains an expiration date within 6 months
  • Expires before December 31, 2026
  • You are responsible for replacing/rotating these keys upon expiration

This is considered an interim solution only.
Non‑compliant keys will be permitted only until December 31, 2026.

4. Work with your configuration manager

Your configuration manager is responsible for maintaining the recipient list for key‑expiration notifications.
If you unexpectedly received the notification, contact your internal configuration manager.
