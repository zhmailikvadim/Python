Symptom

For products for which the valuation payment is calculated according to the interest total method (with real interest or with fictitious interest), the valuation figure is calculated incorrectly for key dates that fall on the account settlement date.

Reason and Prerequisites

In the formula for calculating the valuation figure (BWZ) according to the interest total method with real interest, the total interest of a contract is calculated on a key date by adding two terms: posted interest (INTPST) and mid-year or unposted interest (INTNPS). The term INTNPS determines the value of the interest using the account balance simulation on the key date. If the BWP calculation is carried out for an account settlement date, there is no interest that has not been posted. In this case, the term INTNPS determines the interest that is already contained in the term INTPST again, which leads to an incorrect BWZ.
A similar situation exists for the calculation of the ELT according to the interest total method with fictitious interest, in which the interest total is calculated as the addition of the terms secondary balance fictitious interest (INTFKT) and fictitious interest (simulated) (INTFKS).

Solution

The terms INTNPS (implementing class /IBS/CL_BSC_TERM_INT_NOT_PSTED) and INTFKS (implementing class /IBS/CL_BSC_TERM_INT_VRT_CUR_Y) are adjusted in such a way that they return zero as a result during a calculation for an account settlement date.
