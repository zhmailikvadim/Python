Symptom

The authorization check in the analytical area of TradingContract is not sufficient.

The authorization object W_WBHK_ORG contains the fields EKORG (= PurchasingOrganization) and EKGRP (= Purchasing Group). In the contract, the field EKORG may differ from contract header to contract item and among items. So it is possible that a contract has in its header a purchasing group 001, in item 1 also purchasing group 001, but in item 2 a purchasing group 002 and in item group 3 a purchasing group 005.

In the transactional area this is not taken into account as otherwise the release, especially creating purchase orders, might have issues. A new status 'partially released' with all the resulting issues would then be necessary. This would disrupt the functionality.

In the analytical area this is currently also not taken into account.

This note corrects the behavior in analytical area using the Virtual Data Model, i.e. CDSs.

Items are now returned if

  1. the item has an allowed purchasing group and
  2. the contract header as an allowed purchasing group.

Furthermore, the authorization object F_LFA1_BKA (Supplier Authorization Group) is now also evaluated separately on header and item level, as each item of a Trading Contract may have a different supplier than the header. This differentiation is applied in the analytical area only.

The affected C1 released CDS Views are:

  • I_TrdgContrItem
  • I_TrdgContrItemPartner
  • I_TrdgContrScheduleLine
  • I_TrdgContrItmPurgPrcgElmnt
  • I_TrdgContrItmSlsPrcgElmnt
  • I_TrdgContrItmPurgAssoc
  • I_TrdgContrItmSlsAssoc
