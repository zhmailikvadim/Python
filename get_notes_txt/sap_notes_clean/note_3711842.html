Symptom

Digital Workplace Service (DWS) is a default component in the SAP Build Work Zone advanced and SAP SuccessFactors Work Zone editions.

Why is this migration required?

The DWS migration is required to ensure all components of your SAP Build Work Zone solution run on a single technology platform and that customer data is stored in the same SAP BTP region. This consolidation improves system stability and performance and reduces risks associated with cross-region data management.

Environment

  • SAP Build Work Zone, advanced edition
  • SAP SuccessFactors Work Zone

Resolution

📌 Migration Plan

  • Scope: migration is required for all Work Zone instances where the 'DWS DC' follows the pattern 'DCXX' rather than a region-style identifier, for example- 'eu20-1','us10-1','ap11-1' etc.
    • You can verify if one or multiple of your SAP Build Work Zone environments require this migration. For this, kindly review the 'DWS DC' information listed in Administration Console > Overview of your Work Zone tenant. If this includes the pattern 'DCXX' instead of aforementioned region-style identifiers (e.g. 'eu20-1') the migration is required.
    • All instances matching the DCXX pattern described above are in scope of this migration regardless of environment type (production, preview).
    • Depending on your landscape and implementation timeline, some tenants may require migration while others do not.
  • Process: the migration will involve online data transfer followed by a cutoff window for final adjustments.
  • Downtime: approximately 1 hour of downtime is expected during the cutoff window. This can be scheduled during a planned product maintenance window or at a mutually agreed time slot.

📌 Migration Steps

  1. Customer Pre-Requisites
    • Off-BizX decouple: If your tenant is not already decoupled from BizX, complete the Off-BizX decouple prior to the migration. See SAP Knowledge Base Article 3202787 for more details.
    • SMTP (EU Access Data Center only): If your SAP Build Work Zone instances are hosted in an EU Access Data Center, enabling the integration with your SMTP service is required in advance to ensure that e-mail notifications will continue to work properly. See the related SAP Help Portal page for further details.
  2. SAP
    • Online Data Transfer: Initial data synchronization will occur while system remains online (i.e. available for business users). This step will be carried out by SAP.
  3. Cutoff Window (Downtime)
    • Final data migration and configuration changes will be applied during a cutoff window. Users will be unable to access the system for the duration of this window. This step will be carried out by SAP.
  4. Customer + SAP
    • DNS Update (for Custom Domains): If you use a custom domain for your SAP Build Work Zone environment, you must update the CNAME record for your 'DWS' domain after the migration is complete; see here for further details. The involved SAP team will work closely with your technical contact to ensure a smooth execution.

📌 Customer Impact

  • Service Downtime: The system will be unavailable for approximately 1 hour during the cutoff window. A customer notification event will be sent to your subscribed service and available in SAP4Me. If you have setup your account to receive BTP notifications, then you will also receive the same notification via email.
  • DNS Configuration: If you are using custom domain for your Work Zone tenant, instead of the default workzone.ondemand.com domain, you must update the CNAME record of your custom domain after migration.
  • SMTP Setup: If your SAP Build Work Zone instance is in EU Access DC, SMTP configuration must be completed beforehand to avoid e-mail service disruption.
  • Business Continuity: All user accounts and data will remain intact throughout the migration process. No data loss is expected, ensuring a seamless transition for every user. After migration, your system will run on a unified platform, further improving stability and compliance without affecting existing functionality or data integrity.
