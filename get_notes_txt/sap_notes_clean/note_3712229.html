Symptom

The employer pays compensation for days 1–14, ending at 24:00 on day 14. When the illness is recorded as multiple consecutive entries between days 4 and 14, the 14th day is paid as a full day rather than only until 24:00.

Example:

Illness from 28.11. to 20.12., the 14th day of illness is 11.12., on 11.12. the employee has a night shift from 22:00 on 11.12. to 06:00 on 12.12.

1. Illness entered from 28.11. to 20.12. as a new full-day illness.
The employer's compensation is correctly paid until midnight on 11.12.

2. Sickness entered from 28.11. to 30.11. as a new full-day illness and from 1.12. to 20.12. as a full-day continuous illness.
Employer's compensation is correctly paid until midnight on 11.12.

3. Sickness entered from 28.11. to 30.11. as a new full-day illness, from 1.12. to 3.12. as a full-day continuous illness, from 4.12. to 20.12. as a full-day continuous illness.
Employer's compensation is incorrect for the entire shift from 11.12. (from 22:00 to 06:00) and not just part of it until midnight on 11.12. (from 22:00 to 24:00)

Other Terms

Payroll, HRCZ schema CZT0, HRCZ rule CZ2G, illness
