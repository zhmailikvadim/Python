Symptom

  • The report's run timestamps were postponed, and its success was extended.
  • The SIA and Tomcat went into a stopped state for a few minutes and then started again due to maintenance activity.
  • Not able to see any failed instances even when the SIA and tomcat was stopped.
  • When we schedule a report, it goes to the running state. After that, when we stop and restart the SIA, the report goes to the pending state.

Environment

  • SAP BusinessObjects Business Intelligence 4.x
  • SAP BusinessObjects Business Intelligence 2025

Reproducing the Issue

1. Trigger a report that typically runs for 36-40 hours.

2. During the running state of report, stop SIA and wait for 10 minutes.

3. Start SIA and observe that the report restarts with a new start date. 

Cause

  • The issue occurred because SIA is stopped for few minutes and then restarted again.
  • The Adaptive Job Server (AJS) picks up the job from the pending state and pushed it to the running state again, causing the start time to reset. 

Resolution

  • The parameter called "number of retries allowed" in the recurrence section is AJS is responsible for the running time change of the report
  • If the "number of retries allowed" is configured the job will not get into the failed state until it retries the number of trials given in the "number of retries allowed" parameter.

Keywords

Recurring, delay, schedule
