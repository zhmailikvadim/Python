Symptom

  • The average consumption for material is not determined as expected in transaction MRN1 when the period indicator for this material is set to "W" (weekly).
  • Potential inaccuracy in the calculation of average consumption could be due to the fact that a month typically consists of more than four weeks.

Environment

  • Balance Sheet Valuation Procedures

Reproducing the Issue

  1. Use transaction MRN1.
  2. Select material and plant. For the material, the period indicator is set to "W" (weekly).
  3. Execute the transaction MRN1 and observe the discrepancy in the average consumption value compared to the MVER table.

Cause

Since the weighting factors are only considered on a monthly basis, it is necessary for the program to adjust these factors accordingly. If the consumption is updated weekly (period indicator W), the adjustment is performed using the factor 12/13. This calculation accounts for the assumption that a month consists of four weeks, while a year comprises 52 weeks.

Resolution

Correction factor (12/13) is applied to adjust the weighting factors for weekly consumption data. This adjustment is necessary because the determination of the range of coverage considers weighting factors on a monthly basis. To see the detailed calculation, enable the explanatory tool in the selection screen of transaction MRN1. The adjustment in the calculation can be found in the following line:

Example: Converted Weighting Factors Corrected weight. factor = total weighting factor / 13 * 12 = 36.00 / 13 * 12 = 33.23
