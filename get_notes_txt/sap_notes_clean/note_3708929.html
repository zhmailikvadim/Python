Symptom

Change 2/10/2026: Description added for activating the subapplication MSG2 in the manual activity.

According to Sec. 20 Para. 4 of the Maternity Protection Act (MuSchG), which was supplemented with the law for the extension of the authorization and reduction of bureaucracy in care, the following applies for the supplement to the
Maternity pay to take all statutory deductions into account if the supplement to maternity pay is not subject to tax in the country of residence. This is the case, for example, in Belgium and Switzerland, but not in France.

Up to now, no employment tax was withheld for a cross-border employee in the notional calculation for the supplement to maternity pay.

Other Terms

Schema DZM1, DZM0,  Supplement to Maternity Pay

Reason and Prerequisites

Change to Maternity Protection Act

Solution

Implement the attached correction instructions or import the specified support package. The correction is made using the statutory subapplication MuSchG: Supplement to Maternity Pay for Cross-Border Employees (MSG2), which becomes active in the standard SAP system as of 1/1/2026.

After you import the Support Package, adjust your schemas DZM1 or DZM0 with the standard system. For the manual implementation of this SAP Note, refer to the manual activity.

If you activate this subapplication, the basic data (payroll tables ST and SI) for cross-border employees in the notional calculation of the supplement to maternity pay in accordance with Sec. 20 Para. of the Maternity Protection Act (MuSchG) is adjusted as follows:

  • The system uses tax class 1 for the notional calculation of  the net remuneration for cross-border employees. As a result, the regular gross tax amount is retained in the fictitious runs.
  • In addition, the system sets the SI attribute 31 (No CI Bonus) if,  in the infotype Social Insurance D (0013), the  SI attribute 32 (CI bonus) is not explicitly defined.

Caution
The statutory subapplication  MuSchG: Supplement to Maternity Pay for Cross-Border Employee (MSG2) is active as of 1/1/2026.

Tasks for Processing

Perform retroactive accounting for affected cases up to 1/1/2026.
