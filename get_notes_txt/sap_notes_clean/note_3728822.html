Symptom

  • Address Fields(For Example: 'City') in the Candidate Profile are appearing as Mandatory despite being configured as Non-Mandatory.
  • Address Fields appears as Mandatory when New Candidate Experience is Enabled.
  • Same fields appears as Non-Mandatory when New Candidate Experience is Disabled.

Environment

SAP SuccessFactors Recruiting Management

Reproducing the Issue

  1. Configure fields in the candidate profile as Non-mandatory.
  2. Enable New Candidate Experience.
  3. Navigate to the 'My Profile' page after creating a candidate profile.
  4. Observe that the Address fields(For Example: 'City') appear as Mandatory despite being set as non-mandatory. 
  5. Now Disable New Candidate Experience.
  6. Again, navigate to the 'My Profile' page after creating a candidate profile.
  7. Observe that the Address fields(For Example: 'City') appear as Non-Mandatory as per the configuration.

Cause

Expected system behavior.

When the New Candidate Experience is disabled, the system falls back to the Legacy/Mobile Apply experience, resulting in different field behavior, including mandatory settings and validation rules.

Resolution

Observations and Explanation

1. “City” field appears as Non-Mandatory when New Candidate Experience is Disabled
When the New Candidate Experience (Reimagined Candidate Experience) is disabled in SuccessFactors, the system falls back to the Legacy / Mobile Apply experience.
In this mode, field behavior—including mandatory settings and validation rules—differs from the modern experience, which is why the City field appears as non-mandatory.

2. “City” field appears as Mandatory when New Candidate Experience is Enabled
When the New Candidate Experience is enabled, the City field (along with other address-related fields) becomes mandatory due to Stricter and Modernized validation rules.

This behavior is especially expected if:

  • Candidate data is synced with Employee Central, or

  • Specific career site mappings are configured.

Conclusion

Based on the above findings, the reported behavior is expected system behavior depending on whether the New Candidate Experience is enabled or disabled.
