Symptom

In the standard Story report SF_ONB_I9_E-Verify_Analysis_v2.0, on the page “USCIS Form I-9 and E-Verify Details”, the field Employment Eligibility Expiration Date displays 12/31/9999 for some records (I-9 Section 1).

This may occur even when:

  • Section 1 and Section 2 of the I-9 form are completed, and
  • A valid Employment Eligibility Expiration Date was entered in Section 1.

When reviewing the same employee in Manage Data → I-9 Audit Trail, the same 12/31/9999 value may appear for certain entries.

Environment

SAP SuccessFactors Onboarding

Cause

This behavior occurs because the report retrieves data from the I-9 Audit Trail, which stores entries separately for Section 1 (New Hire) and Section 2 (Employer/Hiring Manager) of the I-9 form in SAP SuccessFactors Onboarding.

Two fields are involved in the I-9 employment authorization expiration process.

Date of Employment Authorization Expiration is populated when the new hire completes I-9 Section 1 and enters their work authorization expiration date. When I-9 Section 2 is submitted by the employer or authorized representative, the system validates the value entered in Section 1 against the document expiration date provided in Section 2. Until Section 2 is completed, the Employment Eligibility Expiration Date field remains with the default value 12/31/9999.

After I-9 Section 2 is successfully submitted, the system updates both the Date of Employment Authorization Expiration and the Employment Eligibility Expiration Date fields with the expiration date of the document entered in Section 2, in I-9 Section 2 task. The Employment Eligibility Expiration Date is then used by the system as the primary date for employment authorization reverification (Note that what triggers I-9 Reverification is the I-9 Section 2 task and its Expiration Dates!).

Resolution

This behavior is confirmed by Onboarding Engineering and Developing team to be working as designed. 

The value 12/31/9999 represents a system default placeholder used when the Section 2 Employment Eligibility Expiration Date has not yet been entered at the time the audit record was created.

Since the Story report SF_ONB_I9_E-Verify_Analysis_v2.0 retrieves data from the I-9 Audit Trail, it reflects the historical entries captured at different stages of the I-9 completion process rather than only the final consolidated value.
