Symptom

Starting from 2026 it is planned to use Unified Monthly Employer Report (JMHZ).

Act No. 323/2025 Coll. introduces the Unified Monthly Employer Report (Jednotné měsíční hlášení zaměstnavatele, JMHZ) in the Czech Republic. This report is designed to centralize and streamline the submission of employer and employee data to public authorities from 01.01.2026 with deferred reporting obligation from 01.04.2026.

This SAP Note contains important information for the transition from 2025 to 2026 with respect to JMHZ, usage of M990, RPCPOJT0 transition from 2025 to 2026 – Retroactive Recalculations and basis for the certificate of no. outstanding debts.

Other Terms

JMHZ, M990, RPCPOJT0

Reason and Prerequisites

Legal change in Czechia was approved and signed by president on 26.08. 2025. It is coming into force on January 1, 2026 with deferred reporting obligation from 01.04.2026.

Solution

Use of M990 in Connection with the Monthly JMHZ Report

Each use of M990 during a retroactive recalculation will result in the creation of a corrective individual and summary report, in which – in addition to the change of the required information – the assessment bases for social insurance and taxes will also be updated.

This fact is fully in accordance with the stated methodology in situations where there is a retroactive reduction of assessment bases to such an extent that (without the use of M990) negative assessment bases would arise in the current period.

The use of M990 is also the current solution for the following situations:

• Unauthorized application of tax benefits for children

• Discount on insurance for employees – only in the case of a subsequent reduction of the discount

• Change of health insurance company retroactively

When using M990 in other situations, we recommend considering the consequences mentioned above, for example, if you perform a retroactive recalculation for an already closed tax period.

RPCPOJT0 Transition from 2025 to 2026 – Retroactive Recalculations

Effective from 1/1/2026, retroactive recalculations with M990 from 2026 to 2025 are stopped in the RPCPOJT0 report in month 12/2025, for which a corrective summary will be generated.

• So, if any correction is made after 1/1/2026 to a period before 1/1/2026 with M990, which affects the assessment base for social insurance, the forwarded difference will be stopped in period 12/2025. For this period, it will be necessary to create a corrective PVPOJ. For example, when correcting from 01/2026 to 12/2025 with M990, there will be a notification in RPCPOJT0 for 01/2026 that it is necessary to generate a corrective report for 12/2025. The Monthly Report within JMHZ (hereinafter referred to as MH) for the period 01/2026 will not include differences from corrections for the period 12/2025.

• In the case of corrections to 2025 without M990, the differences will be forwarded to the current period and will therefore be reflected within the MH. A corrective PVPOJ will not be generated.

There has been no such change for the RPCSPLT0 and RPCPLAT0 reports; they continue to function as before.

Basis for the Certificate of No Outstanding Debts

If an employer requests the relevant territorial social security administration between January 1, 2026, and June 30, 2026, to issue a certificate of no outstanding debts and, as of the date of the request, has not yet submitted the unified monthly report for the months of January, February, and March 2026, they must also provide, along with this request, information about insurance premiums, insurance premium discounts, etc.

We are currently working on a new report that will contain the required data for completing the Overview of Insurance Premiums for these periods on the ČSSZ portal.
