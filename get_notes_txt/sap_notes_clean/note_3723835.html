Symptom

  • In SAP TM, a Carrier Invoice is received with a mismatch between the expected invoice amount and the amount submitted by the carrier. As a result, a Dispute Case is created.
  • The shipper can then review the discrepancy and decide whether to approve or reject the value proposed by the carrier.
  • However, after rejecting the Dispute Case, the Life Cycle Status remains "In Dispute" instead of changing to "Resolved". Additionally, the Action Status changes to "Awaiting Carrier Approval".
  • The user expects the Life Cycle Status to change to "Resolved" after rejecting the Dispute Case.

Environment

SAP Transportation Management Embedded in S/4HANA 1709 and above releases

Reproducing the Issue

  1. Create a Freight Order
  2. Send the freight document to the carrier
  3. In the Carrier Collaboration Portal, the carrier submits a Carrier Invoice to TM
  4. Ensure there is a mismatch between the expected invoice amount and the amount submitted by the carrier
  5. A Dispute Case is created for the Carrier Invoice
  6. Open the Dispute Case and choose "Reject All" to reject the proposed values
  7. Check the statuses after rejection. The Life Cycle Status remains "In Dispute" and the Action Status changes to "Awaiting Carrier Approval"

Cause

  • This is standard system behavior. When the shipper rejects a Dispute Case, the related Carrier Invoice remains open. Therefore, both the Invoice and the Dispute Case are sent back to the carrier for further negotiation.
  • At this stage, the carrier can:
    • Accept the shipper's rejection
    • Propose new amounts for further review.
  • This behavior is stated on SAP Help | Features of Dispute Management
    • Awaiting Carrier Approval:
      • Your service provider must act on a dispute case. This means you as the service requester have taken one of the following actions:
        • Rejected the claim from your service provider. If your service provider agrees with your decision, they must accept the dispute.
        • Proposed a solution that is acceptable to you against the claim from the service provider. If your service provider agrees with your decision, they must accept the dispute.

    Resolution

    • If the shipper does not want to continue negotiations after rejecting the Dispute Case, the Carrier Invoice must also be rejected. This can be done by selecting “Reject Invoice” either in the Invoice document or directly within the Dispute Case.
    • In that case the Invoice and Action Status would be "Completed" and no further action is expected from the carrier.
