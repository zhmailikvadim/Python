Symptom

RFC_SAVE_TEXT can't handle the situation properly when the 132nd character is a space.

Environment

SAP Data Services

Reproducing the Issue

  1. A sample long text: “aaaaaa sample: The aaaaaaer sample is aaaan according to the AAA for inaaaaaon-molded plaaaic parts, VVVVme 22 of the aaatio Caaaor Verlag (latest edition). Raaarence sample: We reaaest storage of 11 samples/batch for 22 years + the comaaaaent to provide this to the AA witaaan 1 woaaaag day.”
  2. By the first dataflow, this long text is split to following 3 pieces. The first two are equal pieces of 132 characters each:
    • "aaaaaa sample: The aaaaaaer sample is aaaan according to the AAA for inaaaaaon-molded plaaaic parts, VVVVme 22 of the aaatio Caaaor " (132characters, the 132nd is a trailing space)
    • “Verlag (latest edition). Raaarence sample: We reaaest storage of 11 samples/batch for 22 years + the comaaaaent to provide this to t”(132 characters)
    • “he AA witaaan 1 woaaaag day” (26 characters)
  3. In the next dataflow calling the FM RFC_SAVE_TEXT. After the call, it’s observed that the space of 132th character is not read so the loaded text is missing that space as following.
    “aaaaaa sample: The aaaaaaer sample is aaaan according to the AAA for inaaaaaon-molded plaaaic parts, VVVVme 22 of the aaatio CaaaorVerlag (latest edition). Raaarence sample: We reaaest storage of 11 samples/batch for 22 years + the comaaaaent to provide this to the AA witaaan 1 woaaaag day.”

Between the “Caaaor” and “Verlag”, it should have a space

 

Cause

The issue is caused by a well-documented ABAP behavior where trailing blanks in character fields are ignored or removed when the data is passed to a function module.

Resolution

There is no workaround from DS since this behavior happens in ABAP, not in DS code.
Customer should avoid passing trailing spaces in the 132nd character to FM RFC_SAVE_TEXT
