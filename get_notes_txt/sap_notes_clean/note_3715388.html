Symptom

When running the report /SHCM/RH_SYNC_BUPA_EMPL_SINGLE to synchronize Employee records to Business Partners, not all available personnel numbers (having multiple assignments) are converted into distinct Business Partners with the employee role. Instead, only one Business Partner with the employee role (BUP003) is created and linked for a central person, although multiple personnel numbers are assigned.

Environment

  • SAP S/4HANA on-premise
  • SAP S/4HANA private cloud edition

Reproducing the Issue

  1. Ensure at least two or more Personnel Numbers (PERNRs) are assigned to the same Central Person (CP), resulting in multiple assignments.
  2. Execute the synchronization report /SHCM/RH_SYNC_BUPA_EMPL_SINGLE.
  3. Observe that only one Business Partner with employee role (BUP003) is created and linked to the Central Person, despite multiple assignments being present.

Cause

This behavior is standard system design. In scenarios with multiple personnel assignments linked to the same Central Person, the synchronization report will only generate one Business Partner with the employee role (BUP003) per Central Person. Although additional Business Partners can be created for vendor-related purposes for the other assignments, only the first Business Partner with the employee role is linked to the Central Person through table HRP1001. It is possible to have multiple CP->P relationships in HRP1001, but only one CP-BP relationship for each Central Person.

Resolution

This is expected system behavior. The report /SHCM/RH_SYNC_BUPA_EMPL_SINGLE creates only one Business Partner with the employee role (BUP003) per Central Person, even if multiple personnel numbers (assignments) are involved. If additional Business Partners are needed for vendor or other roles, they can be created separately. No system action is required unless business requirements dictate a unique approach to dealing with multiple assignments and Business Partners.
