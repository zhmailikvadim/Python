Symptom

Expired SSL certificates are present in the SAP Cloud Integration (NEO) tenant keystore. These certificates were uploaded to the tenant more than eight years ago by an SAP C-User or I-User and were previously used to establish SSL connections. The expired certificates were issued by TC TrustCenter or other certificate authorities.
Examples of affected certificates:
  • TC TrustCenter Class 2 L1 CA XI (TC TrustCenter Class 2 CA II)
  • TC TrustCenter Class 2 CA II

Environment

  • SAP Cloud Integration
  • NEO Environment

Cause

In the early HANA Cloud Integration (HCI) phase, the SAP Cloud Integration Operations team provisioned root and intermediate CA certificates to all Neo-based tenants, reflecting certificate management practices that differed from current standards. These certificates (among them TC TrustCenter CAs) were intended to support HTTPS trust validation by allowing server certificates to chain to trusted authorities in the tenant trust store. As these legacy CA certificates aged, they eventually expired, which is why they are now found as expired entries across Neo tenants.

Resolution

These certificates are no longer maintained by SAP (SAP-managed certificates are identifiable by a lock icon) and are not used anywhere in the service. You can safely remove them from the keystore, or, if you prefer, leave them as-is and ignore the expired entries. For current SSL/TLS connections, use DigiCert-issued certificates.
