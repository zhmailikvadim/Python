Symptom

Capacity Consumption quantity on production orders is being calculated against the original quantity instead of the open quantity.

Environment

SAP Integrated Business Planning

Reproducing the Issue

  1. Open the Orders app.
  2. Display a production order.
  3. Observe that the consumption quantity is calculated against the original quantity instead of the open quantity.

Cause

The observed behavior is an intended design feature.
It is implemented to prevent oversupply situation by ensuring original order quantity is considered when calculating capacity consumption.

 

Why does the system consider the original order quantity?
For example, if the capacity supply for that period is only 2000000 s, and if original quantity of the production order was not taken into account, new supply elements could have been generated because there is still capacity available.
So, once production has consumed the required capacity, it is recorded accordingly to maintain consistency and prevent overallocation. 

In addition, the partial GR dates / quantities with partial capacity consumption are not stored in OBP. Only Original Quantity are used as a basis for capacity consumption calculation.


Resolution

  1. If it is preferred that production orders with zero open quantity do not consume capacity, run the application job "Order-Based Planning: Delete Fulfilled Orders" to clean up such orders.
  2. To propose functionality for differentiating capacity consumption of Goods Receipts quantity and original quantity, submit an improvement request on the IBP Influence Channel for consideration in future product releases. Refer to: IBP Influence Channel.
