Symptom

  • This KBA explains how the system calculates Average Demand Interval (ADI). 
  • It provides details on the calculation methodology, including the rules applied for time series length and demand periods. 
  • The KBA also highlights how leading nulls and trailing nulls are treated during the calculation process. 

"Image/data in this KBA is from SAP internal systems, sample data, or demo systems. Any resemblance to real data is purely coincidental." 

Environment

  • SAP Integrated Business Planning (SCM-IBP)

Reproducing the Issue

  1. Run the Forecast Automation Profile on the specified time series (current time bucket: 26).
     
      24-Mar24-Apr24-May24-Jun24-Jul24-Aug24-Sep24-Oct24-Nov24-Dec25-Jan25-Feb25-Mar25-Apr25-May25-Jun25-Jul25-Aug25-Sep25-Oct25-Nov25-Dec26-Jan26-Feb
    IBP-100ACTUALSPLM - ACTUALSPLM   100     100     100    100   
  2. Verify the Forecast Automation Profile details:

    Historical Periods: 24 Months
    Minimum Required Data Points: 0
    Intermittency Detection Method: Average Demand Interval

  3. Confirm that the ADI calculated by the system is 5.6667

Cause

The following rules are applied:

  • The system subtracts 1 from both the length of the time series and the number of demand periods to calculate the interval between demand periods. 
  • Leading nulls are excluded from the calculation.
  • Nulls after the time bucket 25-Nov are not considered in the calculation. 

Resolution

  1. Identify that there are 18 time periods between 24-Nov and 25-Nov.
  2. Confirm that there are 4 time periods with non-null values.
  3. Calculate the ADI using the formula: (18-1)/(4-1)= 5.6667
