Symptom

You want to transport goods from one US FTZ to another US FTZ in your company or one of your branches using an in-bond transit procedure. Both foreign trade zones use the same SAP GTS system. In the feeder system, you use the functions for a cross-company-code stock transfer in a two-step procedure. To do this, you create a stock transport order, outbound delivery, invoice, and inbound delivery.

In this scenario, you expect it to be possible for the data to be transferred directly from the issuing zone to the receiving zone without having to use the zone-to-zone file.

Note:

Note that SAP GTS only allows you to transfer stock between two zones using the two-step procedure.
You must enter the receiving plant at the point when you create the stock transport order. This is generally an optional field in the purchase order, but SAP GTS requires this information to be able to recognize and map the zone-to-zone process.

Overview of cross-company-code stock transfer process

In the case of a zone-to-zone procedure with the help of a cross-company-code stock transfer, the existing zone-to-zone goods issue
and goods receipt processes are combined in SAP GTS. The zone-to-zone file information is made directly available to the receiving zone without it being necessary to create or upload a file of this kind.
The various process steps for leaving a US FTZ or arriving at a US FTZ remain the same.


For a detailed description of the process, see the new application article on transfer posting finished products or components between FTZ plants published with SP19.
