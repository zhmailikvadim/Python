Symptom

In high availability (HA) deployments, SAP MaxDB liveCache databases are started and managed by HA agents rather than by the SCM application. Consistency checks and post-processing are not part of the HA control plane and cannot be performed automatically at HA startup.

Other Terms

LC10, HA, liveCache start, /SAPAPO/OM17

Reason and Prerequisites

You are using SAP MaxDB liveCache in an HA environment

Solution

In SCM systems, runtime data is split between SAP liveCache and the SCM database. Starting or stopping SAP liveCache independently of the SCM application can produce inconsistencies between SAP liveCache and the SCM database. Other sources of inconsistency include failed background jobs, incorrect SAP liveCache handling, and SAP liveCache crashes. For this reason, regular consistency checks of SAP liveCache are required.

In high availability (HA) deployments, SAP MaxDB liveCache databases are started and managed by HA agents rather than by the SCM application. Consistency checks and post-processing are not part of the HA control plane and cannot be executed automatically at HA startup. Therefore, operators must ensure that consistency checks are performed on these systems.

Note on transaction LC10 alerts: If a SAP liveCache is not started via the transaction LC10 (liveCache assistant), transaction LC10 displays alerts about missing post-processing. According to SAP Note 3727105 - The meaning of the 'Online Without Postprocessing' message in LC10 for MaxDB liveCache, these alerts are informational (non‑critical) for SAP liveCache version 7.9.10.06 and later.
