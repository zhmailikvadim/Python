Symptom

Update on 4/25/2025: SAP Note 3544806 (DaBPV: Delivery of procedure) is now available.

On July 1, 2025, the digital call procedure for the number of children starts for determining the contribution deduction in statutory care insurance. This SAP Note contains information about the notification procedure and the planned delivery of the functions.

As of July 1, 2023, the German Care Support and Relief Act (PUEG) introduced a contribution deduction in statutory care insurance. For more information, see SAP Note 3337857.

The required entry of the number of children had to be made manually first. For the period from July 1, 2023 to June 30, 2025, a simplified verification method has been introduced to reduce the entry effort. According to Sec. 55 Para. 3d SGB XI, the number of children is considered to have been verified in this period if the insured person informs the office deducting contributions about the number of children.

With the introduction of the CI contribution deduction, the introduction of a digital call procedure for the number of children was already decided (Sec. 55 Para. 3c SGB XI). The legal forms for the digital procedure were adopted with the Growth Opportunities Act of March 28, 2024 (Section 55a SGB XI, Section 28a Paragraph 13 Clause 8, Section 124 SGB IV).

If employers have used the simplified verification method for periods as of July 1, 2023, existing employees must not be registered for the digital procedure until July 1, 2025. For the transfer of the registrations, employers have until payroll December 2025 (Sec. 124 SGB IV).

General information on the procedure

The procedure stipulates that employers register their employees within seven days of the start of employment and deregister them within six weeks of the end of the employment. In addition, there are additional deregistration and registration situations during employment. These can occur for personal reasons (removal of CI obligation, change of tax ID) or for organizational reasons (change of main company number, payroll office, or sender).

The following data is transferred with the registration:

  • Identification of the company:
    • Main company number
    • Company number of the office running payroll
    • Sender number
  • Identification of person:
    • Tax ID
    • Date of birth
  • From-date

The from-date determines the date from which the data is to be retrieved. The from-date must be a date in the past. For new entries, the start of employment is to be reported as the from-date (see special features for predictive registrations).

A registration is answered with information about the existence of the parent property and the number of children relevant for the CI contribution deduction. Complete future values are transferred up to the consumption of the number of children to zero.

Example: Confirmation of a registration on July 1, 2025:

  • Parent property: yes (as of July 1, 2025)
  • Children:
    • as of 7/1/2025: 3 children
    • as of 3/1/2029: 2 children
    • as of 11/1/2032: 1 child
    • as of 5/1/2037: 0 children

A registration also sets up a subscription. If the data on the authority side changes at a later point in time, the employer receives a proactive confirmation with the updated data. The proactive confirmations are sent to the employer once a month, usually between the 6th and 10th day of a month (month list).

The subscription is terminated by a deregistration (also known as a cancellation).  The deregistration is confirmed to the employer by a cancellation confirmation. If the employee dies or the tax ID changes, the employer can be notified of a proactive termination of the subscription (cancellation notification).

In addition to the registration, employers can call up the data for a period in the past for individual cases without setting up a subscription. To do this, you use the history request, in which a to-date is to be supplied in addition to the from-date. History requests are answered once with a confirmation - later proactive confirmations are not planned.

The registration office for retrieving the relevant child data is the German Federal Central Tax Office (BZSt). For employers, technical communication takes place via the data office of the statutory pension insurance (DSRV) using the existing interfaces via the DSRV communications server. The DSRV forwards the data to the BZSt using the ZfA. Confirmations from the BZSt return to the DSRV in the same way and are made available to the employers for retrieval.

Paying offices must also register pension recipients with pension payments subject to CI in the DABPV procedure. The same applies to paying offices.

Special conditions:

Predictive registrations

Only a date in the past is allowed as the from-date for registrations. However, the procedure stipulates that registrations can already be transferred if it is possible to foresee that an employment relationship subject to CI will arise. To do this, the common principles for the DABPV procedure apply:

The latest possible from-date is the creation date (request date). This means that a subscription cannot be registered in advance for a period starting in the future. However, in anticipation of an expected employment, pension payment, benefit, membership, insurance obligation, or pension, it is possible and permitted to register the subscription on the current date, provided that a direct deregistration occurs afterwards if this relationship is not formed against expectations.

No predictive registrations

Unlike registrations, deregistrations cannot be performed proactively. Deregistrations do not contain an effective date, but terminate the existing subscription at the time of transfer. This means that deregistrations can only take place after the reason for the subscription has been omitted, otherwise there is a time gap in which no proactive confirmations would be made.

Limitations of the procedure

The BZSt manages the children exclusively for tax purposes and does not plan any additional data storage of the children in the sense of care insurance. Due to the different definitions of the "tax children" and the "CI children", there may be differences between the confirmed data and the actual conditions.

In these cases, the office deducting contributions is authorized and, in contrast to the confirmed data, is obliged to use the data known to it (verified) for the contribution calculation.

Deviations can occur in particular for adoptive children, foster children, stepchildren, and children abroad. In addition, the BZSt has been collecting the data only since the introduction of the ELStAM procedure (start of data collection: 2011). Children who were no longer relevant for tax at this time are not known to the BZSt. This may lead to differences in the confirmation of the parent property for older employees and pension payment recipients.

Planned implementation in the SAP system

The notification procedure is implemented in the familiar context of the SI notification procedure. The specific description of the new reports and information about setting up the procedure is provided at a later point in time with the delivery SAP Note.

The confirmation data is saved in the infotype Electronic Data Exchange (0700) with the new subtype DBPV. A corresponding time slice of the infotype is created for each time slice of the confirmation. In addition to the information about the parent property and the number of children, the new subtype DBPV contains the indicator Payroll-Relevant. This enables you to specify whether or not the data for the number of children is to be used in payroll. The limits of the procedure section describes in which cases the data of the DaBPV procedure cannot be used and alternative child data is to be entered instead. You can continue to use the infotype Family/Related Person (0021) to maintain the alternative child data. It is also still possible to include customer-specific child management using a BAdI implementation.

At the start of the procedure, the existing employees must be registered. During the first confirmation, the system checks the data against the child data already entered in the system. A distinction is made between the following cases:

  • There is no child data in the system. The data from the DABPV confirmation is then transferred automatically and flagged as relevant for payroll.
  • Data on the number of children is available in the infotype Social Insurance D (0013). The system then compares the number of children on the key date of the registration for the DABPV procedure between the DaBPV notification in the entry in infotype 0013. If there is a difference, the case is submitted to the administrator for checking (check case), otherwise automated processing takes place and the data is flagged as relevant for payroll.
  • Child data exists in infotype 0021. The notification data is then compared with the infotype data for the entire period of the notification. If there is a difference, the case is submitted to the administrator for checking (check case), otherwise automated processing takes place and the data is flagged as relevant for payroll.

The decision as to whether a check case occurs in the cases mentioned above and which facts (deviations) are to be processed automatically can be set individually using Customizing and/or BAdI implementation.

The number of children in infotype 0013 is no longer supported after the start of the procedure. As soon as data exists for an employee in infotype 0700 (subtype DBPV), the number of children from infotype 0013 is no longer taken into account in payroll for this period. It does not matter whether or not the data in infotype 0700 is flagged as relevant for payroll. For infotype periods as of 1/1/2026, the field "Number of Children" is hidden in infotype 0013 if no data is contained (value: undeclared).

In the current procedure (existing DaBPV data in infotype 0700), the check case Customizing is no longer important. As a rule, (proactive) confirmations are then transferred automatically. The only exception is that a proactive confirmation is received for employees whose DABPV data is flagged as not relevant for payroll in infotype 0700. In these cases, an administrator must decide how to handle the updated BZSt data.

The administrator makes the decision on check cases in the administrator list. They can decide whether the data in infotype 0700 is to be transferred as relevant for payroll or not relevant for payroll. The data itself is always transferred. The manual change of the subtype DBPV is not supported - with the exception of maintenance of the indicator relevant for payroll. The subtype DBPV therefore always represents the current status of the reporting data. In particular, it is not intended to add additional children by changing subtype DBPV. If alternative child data is used, all relevant children must be entered in the system with birth data.

The information about the existence of the parent property is transferred to all time slices of infotype 0700 (subtype DBPV) that result from the child data. The parent property is determined as before - with subtype DBPV as an additional data source. As a result, a parent property that has already been entered in the system is not overridden by the DaBPV confirmation.

Pilot phase

Employers can enter in the DaBPV procedure already before 7/1/2025. On the basis of Sec. 55a Para. 10 SGB XI, the transfer is permitted as of 4/1/2025. During this pilot phase, the DRV is asking for an advance notification:

As of April 1, 2025, offices deducting contributions and payment offices that use the interface for the employer procedure of the DSRV can participate in the pilot procedure PUEG and transfer initial requests for existing employees or insured persons already before July 1, 2025.
To enable close monitoring and control of incoming data at the DSRV and therefore, avoid system overloads, we want to agree on transfer periods with participants that wish to transfer larger datasets.
We ask all interested parties that wish to transfer datasets of more than 10,000 data records/ 100 data transfers already during the pilot phase to get in touch with the DSRV (rvbea@drv-bund.de) beforehand and make the following specifications:

  • Sender number (ABSN)
  • Company number/payment office number
  • Approximate number of data records/data packages that are to be transferred
  • Contact data contact person

The DSRV will then get in touch with the mentioned contact persons and agree on periods in which the data can be transferred in a controlled manner. Reliable statements about return times cannot be made before the productive start of the procedure.

Delivery 

Delivery by Support Package The advance implementation using an SAP Note with correction instructions is possible with manual effort (import of a UDO report, activation of BC sets, adjustment of Customizing).
