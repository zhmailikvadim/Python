Symptom

With this SAP Note, we address below requirements related to the Expat Regulation:

  • For employees on unpaid or partially paid leave (such as parental, maternity, or adoption leave), the corresponding salary reductions should be taken into account when comparing with the reference income (refer paragraph 19.4.1 of the Handboek Loonheffingen of the Dutch Tax Office). These reductions were previously neglected in Expat Regulation calculations. We have now introduced wage type /3WD (Expat Ded.Min.Ref Income), which can be entered in the Recurring Payments/Deductions (0014) or Additional Payments (0015) infotypes. The amount in /3WD will reduce the basis for wage type /3W- (Ref. Income for 30% Regulation). It ensures that the non-taxable amounts as per Expat Regulation are calculated correctly and employees receives the full benefit. Please pay attention that the amount in /3WD should be calculated and maintained by the employer themselves, for example, via customer specific calculation rules in the payroll scheme. The amount should be stored as a positive amount otherwise it is not taken into account.
  • According to the Dutch tax law, from the month after an employee turns 30, you must use the 'High Reference Income' check in the Fiscal Data NL (0060) infotype. From this point, the employee’s salary must meet or exceed the high reference income threshold. As the employer, you are responsible for maintaining the Fiscal Data NL (0060) infotype for your employees. To help you comply, we have added a check in the Main Payroll Driver (RPCALCN0), which will show a warning message 'Expat Reg: Eligibility based on high income threshold after EE turned 30' if the requirement is not met. The system will also automatically apply the high reference income threshold. It does so by updating the Income Check (CH30P) to 'High Reference Income' in the Payroll Results: Wage Tax (NL) (ST) table.

Other Terms

NSV01, 30% Regulation, HRPAYNL_EN 529, P0060, ST, /3WD, /3W-

Reason and Prerequisites

Corrections in the legal function

Solution

The changes are applicable as of 1st January 2026. After applying the SAP Note into your system, you may need to trigger recalculation for the involved employees.
