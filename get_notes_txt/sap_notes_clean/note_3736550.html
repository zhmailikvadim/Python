Symptom

  • Previously created timesheets can no longer be found.
  • Timesheets that had been in Pending Approval status are no longer visible.

Environment

Production and non-production

Reproducing the Issue

  1. Create a timesheet and leave it in Draft status for more than 10 weeks > observe the timesheet is no longer available.
  2. Create a timesheet and leave it in Pending Approval status for more than 10 weeks > observe the timesheet is no longer available.
  3. Approve a timesheet and do not create an invoice for more than 10 weeks > observe the timesheet is no longer available.
  4. Create a timesheet and do not update it > after 10 weeks from the start date or from the last update, observe the timesheet is no longer available.
  5. Buyer user removes a timesheet, observe the timesheet is no longer available.

Cause

  • The system automatically deletes timesheets when removed by a user or integration.
  • The difference between the timesheet start date and current time exceeds 10 weeks.
  • The difference between the last timesheet update time and current time exceeds 10 weeks.
  • The timesheet is approved and no invoice is created after 10 weeks.

Resolution

  1. Verify whether the missing timesheet met any automatic deletion criteria (removed by user/integration, draft or pending approval older than 10 weeks, no updates for 10 weeks, or approved with no invoice for 10 weeks).
  2. To recreate a new timesheet for the same period, a buyer administrator can push a new timesheet to the worker.
    1. Sign in into SAP Fieldglass > Admin Configuration > Puch Time Sheets
    2. Select the Time Sheet Period (maximum 28 days timeframe) > Select the Worker > Search Time Sheets
    3. Tick the checkbox for the respective timesheet > Push Time Sheets

Note that deleted timesheets are hard deleted, therefore, recovery or audit trail is not available.

To avoid recurrence, ensure timesheets are not left in Draft or Pending Approval for more than 10 weeks and that invoices are created within 10 weeks of approval.
