Symptom

  • If an employee has a KiwiSaver record with a temporary rate reduction (TRR) date earlier than the current pay period end date, the system applies the higher employee and employer contribution rate instead of the lower rate.
  • If an employee has a KiwiSaver record with a TRR date, and the pay period start and end dates fall in March 2026 but the corresponding pay date is on or after April 1, 2026, the TRR date is not considered and the higher contribution rate is applied.

Other Terms

  • Include: PCMASNZ0
  • Report: HNZCALC0
  • Transaction: PC00_M43_CALC
  • Table: T511K
  • Function: P0310

Type of Object Change

  • Workbench Objects – No
  • Code – Yes
  • Schema/Rules – No
  • Wage Types – No
  • Customizing Tables – No

Reason and Prerequisites

  • The current logic determines eligibility for the lower rate by checking whether the TRR date is greater than or equal to the pay period end date.

  • The system retrieves the lower rate from constants KSTRE and KSTRC based on the pay period end date rather than the pay date.

Solution

The logic has been corrected so that the lower rate will be considered if the TRR date is greater than or equal to the pay period start date, and if the current period pay date is greater than April 1, 2026.
