Symptom

Foreign employees in the Czech Republic, particularly employees from country/regions that do not use the Latin alphabet may have multiple name variants:

  • Civil registry Identity ("Matriční identita"): The official Czech transcription of the name as recorded in Czech civil registry documents.
  • Passport identity ("Pasová identita"): The international transliteration of the name into Latin characters as shown in the passport.
  • Original name in non -Latin alphabet

The new JMHZ (Unified Monthly Employer Report) legal requirement mandates that employee names must be reported in a specific format compliant with Czech and Latin characters. The system must allow storing names:

  • Civil Registry Identity for JMHZ legal reporting
  • Passport identity for employee identification and search purposes

Other Terms

HRCZ, IT0002, IT0021, IT0182

Reason and Prerequisites

  • According to Act No. 323/2025 Coll. (Act on the Uniform Monthly Employer Report), employers in the Czech Republic are required to submit the JMHZ report containing employee data in a strictly defined XML format.
  • Under Czech law, the civil registry identity takes legal precedence over the passport identity in official records. Once a Czech civil registry document is registered, the name in official records is the Registry Identity.
  • Civil Registry and Passport Identity
  • This SAP Note is relevant for the Czech Republic only.

Solution

Recommendation for storing employee names when multiple variants exist.

1. Store the Civil Registry Identity in standard name infotypes:

Infotype Usage
IT0002 (Personal Data) Store employee's civil registry identity in the standard name fields
IT0021 (Family Members) Store family member's civil registry identity in the standard name fields

Enter the name exactly as it appears on Czech civil registry documents.

This guideline ensures compliance with the JMHZ XSD character validation.

2. Store the Passport identity in Infotype IT0182 (Alternative Names):

This SAP Note enables the use of IT0182 for Czech localization to store the employee's passport identity. Passport identity can be used for employee identification, search or other business support purpose. Enablement is configured in table T582L for Molga 18.

Usage:

  • Use subtype "2" – Full name as shown in IC/passport to store the passport name.
  • Enter the employee's name as it appears in their passport, the international variant.

Remarks:

  • Search help in PA30(PREM) will be enhanced with field IT0182-ALNAM to ease searching for employee.
