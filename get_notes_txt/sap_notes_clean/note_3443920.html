Symptom

As of August 5, 2022, the record keeping requirements of businesses in India have been increased by virtue of the Companies (Accounts) Fourth Amendment Rules, 2022, issued by The Indian Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MCA)​. The Companies (Accounts) Fourth Amendment Rules, 2022 state that records stored in electronic mode must be accessible in India, at all times, and requires back-up of books of account data in India on a daily basis​. The rules affect all companies operating in India.

For more information, refer to SAP Note 2276890.

Other Terms

India, Companies Act 2013, Section 128; Companies (Accounts) Rules,  rule 3; electronic mode; back-up of the books of account.

Reason and Prerequisites

This SAP Note is valid only for country/region of India.

The books of accounts must be backed up either electronically or physically.

Solution

Customers may consider using the SAP Document and Reporting Compliance, statutory reporting solution to extract relevant books of account data from their SAP S/4HANA Cloud Public Edition.

To guide the user on how to achieve this, the following resources are attached to this SAP Note:

  • A sample custom report created in the SAP Document and Reporting Compliance, statutory reporting solution, with a list of the main files required using standard CDS views available as data sources.
  • Guidelines on how to implement this sample report and extend/improve it, in addition to other resources available for extending and improving your own report.

This note provides a consulting solution. Please follow the steps available in the PDF attachment to implement and configure the custom sample report and the additional recommendations for extensibility capabilities.
