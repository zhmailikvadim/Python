Symptom

This KBA describes recommended best practices for processing large numbers of employees in Malaysia legal reports.

Environment

  • Human Capital Management (HCM)
  • SAP ERP
  • SAP enhancement package for SAP ERP
  • SAP enhancement package for SAP ERP, version for SAP HANA
  • SAP SuccessFactors Employee Central Payroll
  • SAP S/4 HANA

Resolution

  • SAP Note 3707474 provides performance fine-tuning for the EA report. After installing this note, select the checkboxes “Without Printing Breaking List” and “Hide Non-active Period EA Form” to improve performance.

  • It is also recommended to split employees into multiple groups (maximum 200 employees per group) and running the batches sequentially, preferably during off-peak hours. Running the report for all employees at once is not recommended for the following reasons:
    • Massive data volume and I/O: Reading master data, payroll results, and related data for thousands of employees creates high database I/O and large result sets, leading to slow queries and timeout risks.
    • Broad payroll data requirements: The report requires multiple data points and retroactive accounting data, which increases computational workload.
    • High CPU and memory usage: Processing thousands of employees concurrently consumes significant resources, potentially affecting other production workloads.
    • Output and spool pressures: Generating large reports inflates spool/output queues, increases network and storage usage, and risks timeouts or file-system limits.
    • Limits not solvable by simple tuning: These issues are driven by business rules and cannot be fully eliminated through single-point optimizations.
