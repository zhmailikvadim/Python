Symptom

SAP Note 3677874 (YE25: Legal changes effective on January 1, 2026) delivered new rates for constants TSQC1 and TSQC2:

Table T511K:

ConstantEnd DateStart DateValueText
TSQC19999-12-312026-01-019,97Quebec Prov. Sales Tax
TSQC29999-12-312026-01-019,97QST on Taxable Benefits (Rem.)

On the other hand, Revenu Quebec announced that tax rate on insurance premiums will be updated to 9.97% as of January 1st, 2027 (page 223 of Quebec's BUDGET 2025-2026)
This may lead to confusion as one might think that SAP delivered the new rates with wrong start date (2026 instead of 2027).

Environment

  • SAP ERP
  • SAP S/4HANA
  • SAP SuccessFactors Employee Central Payroll
  • HR Renewal

Cause

The update to the rates was done in accordance with Revenu Québec > Tables of GST and QST Rates which states that QST rate is 9.97% as of January 2013.

In fact, constants TSQC* refer to QST, not to insurance premiums. SAP does not deliver a rate for insurance premiums taxation.

These rates work with PCRs KST1 and/or KST2, which state in their documentation (transaction PE02):

"Rule KST1 shows only an example for you to copy to your customer name range. Since the requirements may vary greatly from company to company, you must then modify the copy to suit the needs of your organization."
"As delivered, rule KST2 merely provides a reference model. SAP has not intended to provide a complete solution, since the detailed requirements may differ from client to client."


As of January 1st, 2027, tax rate on insurance premiums will be harmonized with QST rate - both rates will be 9.97%.

Resolution

For the tax rate on insurance premiums, customers may create their own constant in customer namespace for insurance premiums - and adjust the PCR accordingly.

In case they use TSQC1 for tax on insurable premiums, they can manually change the amount in TSQC1 for 2026, until it reaches the same percentage as QST in 2027.
