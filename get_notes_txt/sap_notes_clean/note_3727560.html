Symptom

As part of the electronically supported company audit procedure (euBP), employers must provide the data necessary for the audit electronically: § 28p Abs. 6a SGB IV.

The obligation to submit the audit relevant financial data electronically is in place already since January 1, 2025. But until December 31, 2026, there is a transition phase during which the entrepreneur can request exemption from the obligation to submit electronically.

All details regarding the audit can be found on the official website: Elektronisch unterstützte Betriebsprüfung (euBP)

 

Cause

Legal change

Solution

SAP intends to provide a feature package or patch to solve the symptom that is described in this SAP Note. The section References below lists the related feature package or patch once they become available. Be aware that References can only be confirmed at feature package or patch release date. SAP delivers feature packages or patches only for selected releases at its own discretion, based on the business impact and the complexity of implementation.

To allow for this requirement the following changes are planned for SAP Business One and SAP Business One, version for SAP HANA:

A new data collection run to generate the audit file(s) will be implemented.

The selection of postings to be reported is based on a new parameter, euBP-Relevant, that the user can set in the master data for G/L accounts.

In the Company Details form on the Accounting tab, there is an additional field Company ID (euBP) to enter the company number (BNR) used by the German pension insurance to uniquely identify the company.

It is legally required that the data must be transmitted directly from the accounting system to the institution Deutsche Rentenversicherung. In addition, the software which is used for transmitting the data must be certified by the company ITSG.

Planned delivery of legal change solution: November 30, 2026.
