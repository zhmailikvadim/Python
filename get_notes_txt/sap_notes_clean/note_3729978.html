Symptom

  • Joule is not available or not visible in SAP Field Service Management
  • Uncertainty on how to enable or configure Joule

Image/data in this KBA is from SAP internal systems, sample data, or demo systems. Any resemblance to real data is purely coincidental.

Environment

  • SAP Field Service Management (FSM)
  • SAP Business Technology Platform (BTP)
  • SAP Cloud Identity Services (BTP)
  • SAP Joule

Cause

Joule requires a multi-step setup and integration across several components, including SAP BTP, Cloud Identity Services, system landscape configuration, and proper authorizations (e.g., AI Units and roles).

Resolution

Important Note
This knowledge base article provides a high-level overview and best-practice guidance on how to enable and configure Joule in SAP Field Service Management. It is intended for orientation purposes only and does not replace the official SAP documentation. The complete, and up-to-date configuration steps must always be taken from the official SAP documentation: Integration with SAP Field Service Management | SAP Help Portal 

1. GENERATIVE_AI Role for FSM Account

To enable and use Joule in SAP Field Service Management, it is required that the FSM Account has the appropriate authorizations, specifically the GENERATIVE_AI role, and that the necessary AI Units are purchased.

The GENERATIVE_AI role is mandatory for using Joule functionalities. While it is technically possible to configure and set up Joule without this role being assigned, the actual usage will be significantly limited. In such cases, only basic informational use cases—such as searching documentation—are available, while the core AI-powered features of Joule cannot be utilized. Therefore, assigning this role is essential for full use of Joule.

The assignment of the GENERATIVE_AI role is directly tied to the purchase of AI Units. This role is automatically assigned once the required AI Units have been successfully purchased and activated. Without a valid AI Unit subscription, Joule cannot be fully used, as the premium AI-driven functionalities remain unavailable.

Customers who want to purchase AI Units should:

  • Contact their SAP Account Executive. If the responsible SAP contact is unknown, create a support ticket (component CEC-SRV-FSM-AI) requesting assistance in identifying the appropriate contact.
  • If you would like to purchase the Joule SKU, please visit the official SAP webpage: Joule base  

Additional details on available AI functionalities and the required authorizations can be found in Required Authorizations for AI Functionalities | SAP Help Portal 

2. SAP BTP Subaccount

To enable Joule in SAP Field Service Management, an active SAP BTP subaccount is required.

Joule is an embedded AI application integrated across SAP products, and therefore relies on the underlying SAP Business Technology Platform infrastructure. As a result, the subaccount must be created and configured in a data center that is supported by Joule.

Therefore, when creating or selecting a subaccount, ensure that:

  • The subaccount is active and accessible
  • The region (data center) is supported by Joule

For more information, refer to the SAP Help Portal: Data Centers Supported by Joule | SAP Help Portal 

3. Cloud Identity Services and SSO Configuration

Within your SAP BTP subaccount, the Cloud Identity Services application must be available and properly configured.

Joule requires that your SAP Field Service Management system is integrated with SAP Cloud Identity Services (IAS) and that authentication is handled via Single Sign-On (SSO). This means that users must be able to log in to SAP FSM using IAS before proceeding with any further configuration.

It is therefore a mandatory prerequisite to ensure that:

  • Cloud Identity Services is enabled in your BTP subaccount
  • SAP Field Service Management is configured for SSO with SAP IAS
  • Users are able to successfully log in to FSM via SSO

The official SAP documentation provides guidance on the prerequisites and setup steps: Federated Authentication | SAP Help Portal 

In addition, the SAP Knowledge Base Article 0003521956 offers detailed instructions on how to configure Cloud Identity Services together with SAP Field Service Management, with the goal of enabling SSO login to FSM. This KBA can serve as a practical reference and provides valuable insights into the required configuration steps.

Ensuring a working SSO setup is essential, as it forms the foundation for further configuration.

4. Enable Intelligent Filtering

As part of the prerequisites, it is required to enable Intelligent Filtering.

To ensure a correct setup, follow the guidance provided in the official SAP documentation.

For more information, refer to: Enable and Use Intelligent Filtering | SAP Help Portal

5. Configure Trust to the Identity Authentication Tenant

As part of the setup, it is required to establish trust between your SAP BTP subaccount and the Identity Authentication tenant (SAP IAS).

This trust configuration ensures that authentication requests are securely handled via SAP Cloud Identity Services, enabling proper integration and communication between the involved systems.

The official SAP documentation provides detailed step-by-step instructions on how to configure this trust relationship, including all required settings and dependencies.

For further details, refer to: Configure Trust to the Identity Authentication Tenant | SAP Help Portal 

6. System Landscape Discovery in SAP BTP

As a prerequisite for running Joule Booster, it is required that both your SAP Field Service Management system and your Cloud Identity Services tenant (SAP IAS) are discovered in the SAP BTP system landscape.

This means that both systems must be visible under:

  • Global Account → System Landscape → Systems in SAP BTP

This step is essential because during the completion of the Joule setup (e.g., when running the Joule booster), you will be required to select these systems. If they are not available in the system landscape, the configuration cannot be completed.

Therefore, ensure that:

  • Your SAP FSM system is properly registered/added and visible in the system landscape
  • Your SAP Cloud Identity Services (IAS) tenant is also available and discoverable

You can also refer to the prerequisites section in the official documentation for further guidance on how to onboard and register these systems: Integration with SAP Field Service Management | SAP Help Portal 

7. Run the Joule Booster

To complete the setup and integration of Joule with SAP Field Service Management, it is required to run the Joule booster in SAP BTP.

The Joule booster automates the creation of the Joule application and performs the necessary configuration steps within your SAP BTP subaccount. To run the booster, the appropriate entitlements for Joule must already be assigned to your subaccount.

The official SAP documentation provides detailed guidance on how to run the Joule booster and what prerequisites must be fulfilled beforehand.
For more information, refer to: Integration with SAP Field Service Management | SAP Help Portal 

Additionally, it is recommended to review the following documentation sections:

  • Initial Setup | SAP Help Portal
  • Prerequisites | SAP Help Portal

Depending on your landscape, there may be different setup scenarios. For example, if Joule has already been partially configured or if a Joule application already exists in your SAP BTP subaccount, the required steps may vary. Therefore, always consult the official documentation to determine the exact procedure for your specific scenario.

If you are performing the Joule setup for the first time, you can typically follow the standard approach described in the documentation (Integration with SAP Field Service Management | SAP Help Portal ). In this case, running the Joule booster will automatically create the Joule application and guide you through the required configuration steps.

8. SAP BTP Destination Attribute

As part of the setup, a destination named FSM_SSO should be automatically created in your SAP BTP subaccount. 

It is required to verify that this destination is correctly configured, in particular the attribute settings.

Pay special attention to the following:

  • Open the destination FSM_SSO in your SAP BTP subaccount
  • Check the attribute Audience
  • Ensure that the Fixed Value of this attribute matches your FSM account name

For further guidance, refer to the official SAP documentation section: 2. Update the SAP BTP destination attribute

9. Configure Joule User Attributes in the Identity Directory

It is required to configure the necessary user attributes in the Identity Directory. For detailed instructions and the required attribute configuration, refer to the official SAP documentation:

  • Configure User Attributes for Joule from the Identity Directory | SAP Help Portal

10. Results and Verification

Once you completed the integration steps, the following will be created for you:

  • A system landscape formation including Joule and the SAP Field Service Management system that you select for the integration.
  • A subscription to Joule.
  • A SAP BTP destination titled FSM_SSO to enable the communication from Joule to SAP Field Service Management.
  • A client titled -sap-joule in your SAP Field Service Management account.
  • A SAP Field Service Management SAML configuration to establish trust for the SAP BTP destination.
  • A SAP Field Service Management account configuration titled sap.joule that references your Joule application endpoint.

For further details, refer to the official SAP documentation: Results

Verify the Results

After the integration process is complete, Joule should be available in your SAP Field Service Management system and be ready for use. To verify this, log in to your account and check whether the Joule icon appears in the upper right part of your Shell bar.
