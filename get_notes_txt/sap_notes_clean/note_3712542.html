Symptom

There may be an understanding that withholding taxes, such as INSS, PIS, COFINS, CSLL, and IRRF, should be excluded from the IBS/CBS calculation base.

Environment

  • SAP ERP
  • SAP S/4HANA

Resolution

According to our Legal Product Managers:

"IBS and CBS do not make part of the withholding taxes base amounts. However, the withholding taxes cannot be excluded (subtracted) from IBS and CBS base amounts.

SAP system can only exclude some value from the tax calculation base if there is an express legal provision. "Express" because it cannot be by analogy or a deduction (opinion) of the taxpayer. The values that will be excluded from the calculation bases of the IBS (Goods and Services Tax) and the CBS (Contribution on Goods and Services), according to the general rule, are those indicated in Article 12 of Complementary Law No. 214/2025. If there is no provision for exclusion from the calculation base, it cannot be carried out.

The legislation does not establish the exclusion of the “withheld taxes” from the IBS/CBS bases of calculation but rather those of consumption taxes that are included in prices and are expressly indicated. The legislation does not mention the Social Security Contribution (INSS), Income Tax, or the Social Contribution on Profit. And if there is a case for exclusion in some specific situation, it must be mentioned. Withheld taxes are not exactly the taxes owed by the supplier that have been included in the operation value. These amounts are advance payments of taxes that the supplier will have to collect. Regarding PIS and COFINS, these are the amounts related to the "amount levied in the operation." And "levied in the operation" is not the withheld amount because withholding may or may not occur, and the percentage can be different (it's only the same if the company is under the Presumed Profit taxation regime).

Providing a numerical example for a company under the Real Profit regime (just to keep it different): Service value: R$ 10,000.00
ISS value (5%): R$ 500.00
PIS value (1.65%): R$ 165.00
COFINS value (7.6%): R$ 760.00

IBS and CBS calculation base: R$ 8,575.00

Values that would be withheld:
PIS (0.65%): R$ 65.00
COFINS (3%): R$ 300.00
If these values were excluded, the calculation base would be larger (and would be wrong). These values do not correspond to what is due in the operation and that are included in the price. Therefore, this is not the criterion for exclusion from the calculation base. It is not in the law the exclusion of both amounts: the due and the withheld.

If it's a supplier under the cumulative regime (which generally is Presumed Profit): Service value: R$ 10,000.00
ISS value (5%): R$ 500.00
PIS value (0.65%): R$ 65.00
COFINS value (3%): R$ 300.00

IBS and CBS calculation base: R$ 9,135.00
In this example, the withheld and deducted values would be equal, but this is not the rule. And there is the ISS, which, regardless of being withheld, is deducted from the calculation bases of IBS and CBS."

In short: withholding amounts represent advance payments and do not reduce the IBS and CBS tax bases, even if, in some regimes, the withheld and due amounts happen to be the same.
