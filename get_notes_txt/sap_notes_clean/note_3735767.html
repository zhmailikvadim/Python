Symptom

  • There is an issue with implementing Complementary Law No. 224/2025, introducing new rules for the granting and reduction of tax, financial, and credit incentives by the Federal Government.
  • Confirmation is needed on whether SAP provides a solution for this requirement.

Environment

  • Localization Brazil
  • SAP ERP
  • SAP S/4HANA

Cause

Legal requirements for PIS, COFINS and IPI

Resolution

The solution for using PIS and COFINS with 4 decimal places has been available for years, utilizing internal codes: PISRATE4DEC for condition type BPI3 and COFINSRATE4DEC for condition type BCO3. 

For documentation or SAP Notes related to "PISRATE4DEC" or "COFINSRATE4DEC," refer to:

  • SAP Note 1645721 - Maintain tax rates with 4 decimal places for PIS and COFINS - released in 2012
  • SAP Note 1713458 - PIS/COF Rates with 4 Decimal Places in MM/SD: Condition Based Taxes - released in 2012
  • SAP Note 1699986 - PIS/COF Rates with 4 Decimal Places in MM/SD: Customizing Views - released in 2013
  • SAP Note 1853059 - Customizing for PIS/COF Rates with 4 Decimal Places in MM/SD - released in 2013

If there are issues related to the above solution when using PIS and COFINS in BRL currency with 4 decimals, it is consider a consulting matter. Evaluation should be done with your local consultants team or a specialized consultancy of your choice.

SAP recently released a solution for Complementary Law 224/2025 that includes the IPI tax rate with 4 decimals. Additionally, a fix for PIS and COFINS in foreign currency with 4 decimals on the NFe mapping was released.

This is detailed in SAP Note 3728937: Complementary Law 224/2025: IPI tax rate with 4 decimal places on RVABRA.

Within the note, the symptom provides links to the Regulatory Change Manager information for each release. Please carefully review all contents in the "Solution" section and check the attachments before implementing this SAP note.  Please ensure to read all detailed contents in the "Solution" section and review the attachments carefully before implementing this SAP note. 

For SD, the solution differs between TAXBRA/RVABRA (CBT) and TAXBRJ/RVXBRA (Classic):

  • CBT involves a new condition created by the customer, with the rate entered in VK11 multiplied by 100 (e.g., 12.34 entered in VK11).
  • Classic uses a BAdI to determine the rate up to 4 decimal places without multiplying by 100 (e.g., 0.1234 entered in the BAdI) and does not involve a new condition in pricing.

In both cases, the rates are mapped to the field with 4 decimal places (J_1BNFSTX):

  • CBT uses mapping configuration for NF.
  • Classic uses coding.
