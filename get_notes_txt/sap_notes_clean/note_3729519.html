Symptom

During the third and fourth week of an employee illness, the Belgian national health insurance service (RIZIV/ INAMI) pays a portion of an employee's limited wage; the employer completes the remaining payment based on the employee's normal taxable income.

The constants involved in limited wage calculations change annually, as determined by the RIZIV/ INAMI. Therefore, system changes are required to update the constants involved and ensure that guaranteed wage calculations are accurate.

The limited wage has been increased as of 01.03.2026.

The Payroll Parameters SVZ5D and SVZ6D have been updated the last time via SAP Note 3570714 in 2025. 

Other Terms

Illness, guaranteed wage, wage limit

Note

In order to calculate an employee's guaranteed wages, various payroll constants are required. The system uses the constants to calculate the employer portion of an employee's guaranteed wages, as follows:

  • Daily wage limit
    The daily wage limit is a reference value that is compared with the employee's normal daily wage.
    The daily wage limits are determined by the Belgian authorities and change annually. The daily limits are controlled by two constants in the Payroll Parameters (V_T511P) Customizing view, as follows:
  • SVZ5D - Wage limit illness (5d regime)
  • SVZ6D - Wage limit illness (6d regime)

                       Note: The wage limit constants contain the legal value rounded to two decimal places.

  • Difference between the daily wage limit and the employee's normal daily salary
    The difference is multiplied by a percentage value to determine the employer payment. The percentage applied depends on whether the difference is under or over the daily wage limit, and whether the employee is a blue or white collar worker.
    The percentage values are defined in four constants in the Payroll Parameters (V_T511K) Customizing view, as follows:
  • SVAOL - %guar. under wage limit (blue)
  • SVBOL - %guar. under wage limit (wht)
  • SVABL - %guar. over wage limit (blue)
  • SVBBL -  %guar. over wage limit (wht)

The B014 personnel calculation rule uses these constants in calculations to determine guaranteed wages.
