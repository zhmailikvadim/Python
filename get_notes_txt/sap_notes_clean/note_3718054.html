Symptom

The Government of India has proposed a new Income-tax legislation intended to replace the Income-tax Act, 1961, which is proposed to come into force from 1 April 2026 (financial year 2026–27). The draft legislation includes a renumbering and restructuring of tax provisions, along with mapping of existing sections to new section numbers.

Once enacted and made effective, this change may impact:

  • Withholding tax calculations in FI-CA
  • TDS reporting
  • Withholding tax certificates and statutory forms
  • Compliance configurations in SAP systems

Reason and Prerequisites

Reason:

Legal requirement due to enactment of new Income-tax Act, 2025 in India, which supersedes the Income-tax Act, 1961.

Prerequisites:

- SAP S/4HANA with India localization (Country Version IN)

- FI-CA component activated for withholding tax

- Appropriate authorization for customizing maintenance

Solution

SAP provides standard configuration capabilities to handle this legal change through time-dependent customizing tables.

Steps to Implement - just a CONSULTING, no correction instructions to implement:

1. Access Configuration:

   - Transaction: SM30

   - View: FKKID_WHTSP_V

2. Create New Time-Dependent Entries:

   - Set validity start date as per IT Act 2025 effective date

   - Update the section field with new section numbers as per Section 393(1) mapping

   - Maintain appropriate threshold values

3. Mapping Reference:

   - Refer to Section 393(1) of IT Act 2025 for official old-to-new section mapping

   - Common mappings include:

     - Old Section 194A → New Section under IT Act 2025

     - Old Section 194C → New Section under IT Act 2025

     - Old Section 194H → New Section under IT Act 2025

     - Old Section 194I → New Section under IT Act 2025

     - Old Section 194J → New Section under IT Act 2025

     - (Refer to official government notification for complete mapping)

 For detailed configuration steps, refer to SAP Help Portal: Tax Deducted at Source for Security Deposits.
