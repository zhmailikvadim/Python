Symptom

Unable to create or edit LTSD due to error message - "The validity of the LTSD must not exceed &"
(Error message /SAPSLL/PRE_LTSD112)

Environment

  • SAP Global Trade Services Edition for Hana
  • SAP Global Trade Services (GTS)

Reproducing the Issue

  1. Open app Manage Long-Term Supplier's Declaration
  2. Create a new LTSD
  3. For the field 'Valid to' date, enter a date for example where the validity period is more than 1 year.
    Example: 01.01.2024 to 31.12.2026
  4. Receive error -> "The validity of the LTSD must not exceed &"
    (Error message /SAPSLL/PRE_LTSD112)

Cause

Review the current settings in your SAP Global Trade Services (GTS) system to ensure they align with the Union Customs Code (UCC) regulations, which permit long-term vendor declarations (LTVDs) for example, for a maximum period of two years for future periods and one year for past periods. 

Resolution

For the DOO (Document of Origin) type, the setting 'Max.Validity' is set to 12 months. 

Change this to 24 months and then retest.

See path : SPRO > SAP Global Trade Services, edition for SAP HANA > Preference Management > Supplier's Declarations > Documents of Origin > Define Documents of Origin

For example, the field 'Max.Validity' (Maximum Validity Period of Document of Origin in Months) determines the maximum allowed validity period of the long-term supplier’s declaration. 
The maximum validity period is checked both when creating the LTSDs for suppliers and when issuing LTSDs for customers. This constraint is also used when predefining the validity period for automatically-created LTSDs for customers. 

The setting in the customizing below relates to the administrative unit level. 

See path : SPRO > SAP Global Trade Services, edition for SAP HANA > Preference Management > Supplier's Declarations > Define Settings for the LTSD Process.

Select the relevant administrative unit and display the administrative unit setting in detail.
For the field 'Max. Calendar Years for Issue', enter the relevant period in months. For example, in accordance with the new UCC regulation, maintain the value '24 months'.
