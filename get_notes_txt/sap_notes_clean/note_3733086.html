Symptom

Organizations using US Payroll might experience issues where wages earned in other states are wrongly credited for unemployment insurance purposes due to default settings.
This can lead to non-compliance with specific state regulations that require selective deactivation of such crediting.

This behavior may also happen for the tax types 7, 8, 13, [...], 18, 36, and 39 that are processed in a manner similar to 'tax type 10' for employees who have relocated.
As explained on the SAP Note 326043 - RCP2000: Tax typs. 7,8,13,14,15,16,17,18,28,36,39 processing

Environment

  • HR Renewal
  • SAP S/4HANA
  • SAP ERP  
  • SAP SuccessFactors Employee Central Payroll

Reproducing the Issue

  1. Run payroll for employee with wages in multiple states and verify unemployment insurance tax calculations;
  2. Validate that out-of-state wages;
  3. Check payroll log (USTAX function) to confirm proper processing of insurance data;

Cause

The standard system configuration automatically activates the crediting of out-of-state wages for unemployment insurance purposes.
Without customized configuration for each state in accordance specifics business scenarios, this default setting may lead to non-compliance, as different states have varying regulations on wage crediting.

Resolution

To address the issue of incorrect out-of-state wage crediting for unemployment insurance in US Payroll, begin by verifying the feature 10OSW for your specific state conditions. 
Access the feature editor using transaction PE03, and modify feature 10OSW to set up decision tree logic that aligns with your organization's requirements and adheres to state regulations. This involves configuring return values to manage wage crediting based on various factors like work state, employee subgroup, or tax types.

Next, conduct thorough testing with payroll scenarios that reflect different states to ensure accurate unemployment insurance tax application.
Validate whether out-of-state wages are being included or excluded correctly as per the configured feature.

Use Cases Instances:

Scenario 1: Multi-State Operations with State-Specific Rules

  • Description: Company has employees working in multiple states, each with different unemployment insurance crediting regulations
  • How feature 10OSW applies: Feature decision tree is configured to deactivate crediting for specific states that do not allow out-of-state wage transfers
  • Expected outcome: Unemployment insurance taxes are calculated correctly for each state according to their specific regulations

Scenario 2: Tax type 008 for Louisiana

  • Description: Wages paid to an employee covered under the unemployment insurance law of another state do not count toward the Louisiana taxable wage base.
  • How feature 10OSW applies: Feature decision tree is configured to deactivate crediting for specific the specific tax type that will not allow out-of-state wage transfers.
  • Expected outcome: Taxes are calculated correctly for the state according to their specific regulations;

Regular monitoring and updates are crucial to maintaining compliance with evolving state unemployment insurance laws. This proactive approach will help ensure accurate unemployment insurance reporting and compliance across multiple states.
