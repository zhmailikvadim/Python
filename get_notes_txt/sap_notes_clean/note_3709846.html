Symptom

What does the “Final Form” date in the Job Requisition Audit Trail represent?

Environment

SAP SuccessFactors Recruiting Management

Reproducing the Issue

  1. Open any job requisition.
  2. Click the Info icon to display the Audit Trail.
  3. Observe the "Final Form" date in the audit entries.

Resolution

The "Final Form" date represents the Form Due Date for the job requisition approval process.

  • For Job Requisitions created from Recruiting: The Form Due Date is manually entered on the Short Requisition Create Page.
  • For Job Requisitions created from Position Org Chart: The system calculates the Form Due Date based on the configuration in Admin Center -> Form Template Settings.

Editing the Form Due Date:

If the "formDueDate" field is defined and permissioned in the Job Requisition template, approvers can modify the Form Due Date directly on the Job Requisition page during the approval process.

Once the requisition is fully approved, the field becomes read‑only, regardless of permissions.

For more information, refer to the KBA 2448421 - How to change the Due Date in a job requisition after you entered the date on the short requisition create page? - Recruiting Management.

Customizing the Label Translation:

To customize the translation of the "Final Form" label, use Manage Languages tool to update the message key: AUDIT_TRIAL_completed

For more information, refer to the KBA 2576546 - Using the Manage Language Feature to Change Labels in SuccessFactors
