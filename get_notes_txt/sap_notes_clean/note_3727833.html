Symptom

Ensuring precise gross-up calculations in US payroll can be challenging. Discrepancies may arise when the calculated gross amount fails to deliver the specified net payment accurately.

Environment

  • HR Renewal
  • SAP S/4HANA
  • SAP ERP  
  • SAP SuccessFactors Employee Central Payroll

Reproducing the Issue

  1. Enter the guaranteed net amount into the employee's master data in SAP payroll, for instance IT0015.
  2. Process payroll and observe the gross-up calculation in the USTAX function.

Cause

Discrepancies in the gross-up process may result from incorrect or missing configuration settings. Furthermore, confusion between cash and non-cash gross-up types can lead to processing errors, particularly if non-cash gross-ups are not properly configured. It's also crucial to recognize that SAP does not currently provide model wage types for non-cash gross-ups.

Resolution

To achieve accurate gross-up calculations in SAP payroll:

  1. Configuration Check:
    • Ensure that wage types intended for gross-up are correctly marked with Processing Class 68 set to value 5 for the regular method or value 6 for the supplemental method, depending on the payment type. These values guide the system in determining which gross-up method to employ.
  2. Cash Gross-Up:
    • Verify that cash gross-up calculations include accumulating the grossed-up amount into /101, which corresponds to typical cash bonuses or expatriate payments. To ensure the grossed amount is accurately reflected in payroll results.
  3. Non-Cash Gross-Up:
    • Establish configurations for non-cash gross-ups where no direct cash payment is received. Use three distinct wage types:
      • The guaranteed net,
      • The grossed-up amount,
      • A third wage type representing the tax difference.
    • The third wage type should be correctly linked via table T512W to store the tax computation on the benefits or other taxable items.
  4. Tax Processing:
    • Review the configuration of payroll function USTAX, which processes gross-up calculations before transitioning to gross-to-net wages.
  5. Deductions Management:
    • Ensure deductions are not interfering with gross-up calculations or reviews during payroll processing, as gross-ups are calculated without considering additional deductions, as for instance pre-tax deductions;

Kindly keep in mind that each gross up payment to be processed in the pay period is done in a separate BSI call. Hence, it is indicated to conduct thorough testing using independent payroll periods to validate that gross-up calculations are returning expected outcome
