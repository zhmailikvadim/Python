Symptom

  • When using a customer reference number with leading zeros, these are removed when the transportation Requirement is saved in Transportation Management (TM).
  • Leading zeros are removed during XML mapping.

Environment

  • SAP Transportation Management 9.6
  • SAP S/4HANA 2020 and above releases

Reproducing the Issue

  1. Send a customer reference number through delivery notes, confirmation messages or other XML services from ECC to the Transportation Management system.
  2. Observe that the leading zeros are removed from the saved data (e.g. Customer Reference '001' is saved in TM as '1')

Cause

  • The discrepancy is caused by a standard conversion process, CONVERSION_EXIT_ALPHA_OUTPUT, which aligns ECC and TM fields during integration.
  • The XML mapping process removes the leading zeros due to specific historical and technical requirements embedded in the code architecture.

Resolution

To maintain the customer reference in its original format:

  • For embedded scenarios:
    • Use the BAdI /SCMTMS/BADI_LOGINT_CHG_TTRQ to alter the data coming from the ECC side.
    • Alternatively, use the BAdI of the TPNOR interface and retrieve this information directly from table VBKD - BSTKD during the mapping.
  • For side-by-side integration scenarios:
    • Apply adjustments using the BAdI /SCMTMS/TRQ_IF_SE_REQREQ
