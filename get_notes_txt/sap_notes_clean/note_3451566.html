Symptom

This KBA explains the current system logic about only 'Male' and 'Female' can be used as gender for Employees and Family Members in Malaysia solution.

Environment

  • Human Capital Management (HCM)
  • SAP enhancement package for SAP ERP
  • SAP enhancement package for SAP ERP, version for SAP HANA
  • SAP SuccessFactors Employee Central Payroll

Resolution

Currently, except for the forms and download files generated by the CP22 - Notification Form by Employer for New Employee (RPCT22L0) report, none of Malaysia's legal forms or downloadable files need to indicate an employee's gender. The payroll driver does not check the gender of the employee either. However, the 12th digit of the Malaysian National Registration Identity Card Number (NRIC Number) is used to indicate the holder's gender, and the last digit of the Malaysian Income Tax Number (TIN) is used to indicate whether the taxpayer is a husband or wife. 

To maintain data consistency, it is not allowed to add any gender other than male and female into the Gender field on the Personal Data Infotype (0002) and Family Member/Dependents Infotype (0021) screens. Additionally, the value set of P0002-GESCH and P0021-FASEX cannot be modified using the V_T77PAD_GENDER view.

  • Infotype 0021

The Malaysian National Registration Identity Card Number (NRIC Number) is a unique 12-digit number issued to Malaysian citizens and permanent residents. It is used by the IRBM to identify taxpayers and is provided by the National Registration Department of the Malaysian Government. The format of the NRIC Number is YYMMDD-PB-####, where the first six digits represent the date of birth, followed by a dash (optional), a two-letter place of birth code, another dash (optional), three random digits, and a one-digit gender code. The final digit is even for females and odd for males.

The Malaysian Income Tax Number (TIN) is 12 or 13 characters long and follows the format AA NNNNNNNNNNN, where the first one or two letters indicate the type of individual or entity. Both individuals and entities registered as taxpayers with the Inland Revenue Board of Malaysia (IRBM) are assigned a TIN. The last digit of an individual's TIN is either 0 or 1, with 0 representing the husband's TIN and 1 representing the wife's TIN. If a TIN is not available, the Malaysian National Registration Identity Card Number (NRIC Number) can be used as a functional equivalent for individuals.

Usually, both the NRIC number and TIN remain the same throughout the taxpayer's entire life.

Note: On screens 2014 or 2114 of the Personal Data (0002) infotype, as well as on screens 2014, 2114, or 2214 of the Family Member/Dependents (0021) infotype, the gender field is displayed as a radio-button group with two options. To fulfill the customer-specific requirements, it is feasible to configure the value set of P0002-GENDER_SI (Self-Identified Gender) by accessing view V_T77PAD_GEND_SI. Subsequently, a customizing screen for the Personal Data (0002) infotype can be developed.
