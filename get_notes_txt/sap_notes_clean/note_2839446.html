Symptom

You are wondering about why processing speed performance problem(s) might occur in large document with many items.

Environment

  • Materials Management (MM)
  • SAP ERP Central Component
  • SAP ERP
  • SAP enhancement package for SAP ERP
  • SAP enhancement package for SAP ERP, version for SAP HANA
  • SAP S/4HANA

Cause

The overall system performance is affected/influenced by many contributing factors like, e.g., available server memory, workprocess configuration, timeout settings, available parallel processing, hardware configuration in general, etc. But no matter how advanced all these influencing factors are configured on your system side, there is no doubt whatsoever that the creation of a purchasing document with a few thousand items will never be performant within an SAP standard system.

Resolution

As a natural consequence, SAP strongly recommend NOT to create such large documents at all. This holds even more that given such a large document (no matter whether it is an RFQ, PR, PO, or contract), you will be facing SEVERE performance implications when it comes to the processing of follow-on documents posted against the original ones.

Please keep in mind that every item may give rise to the creation of several (i.e., more than one) follow-on documents along the complete business process. And in such a case, you will not be able to handle the document history anymore in case you do not only have 10.000items in the document itself, but also multiple times 10.000 follow-on documents created and posted against them.

Our official recommendation is to create / use documents with no more than 500-1.000 items at most*, depending on the complexity of the referring items. The more complex they are (with respect to contained data), the less should be their number. At such a size, your system will always show reasonable performance in every transaction, therefore we always suggest this type of process to all of our customers.

Larger existing documents should be split into smaller ones, wherever possible ('item split'). You should also take into consideration that there are further TECHNICAL constraints to large document handling in standard SAP systems, e.g., invoice posting with transaction MIRO only allows for the simultaneous posting of 999 invoice lines anyway due to field length aspects.

* this is a general recommendation primarily intended for purchase requisitions, orders, and contracts that have direct links to their final follow-on documents. Documents may cross reference others with a large volume of historical data, meaning these guidelines may need to be applied even more cautiously. For example, creating a goods receipt from an inbound delivery with relatively few items can still be performance-intensive if it references purchase orders with extensive history. These scenarios should be evaluated on a case-by-case basis, considering the specific data volumes and processing steps involved.
