Symptom

During BOM3 ballooning, warnings with number "027" are raised although balloon positions are determined correctly.

This can occur if a part is used both in a top-level assembly and in a subassembly with split positions.
Warnings are reported for part instances belonging to subassemblies, even though the corresponding balloon positions are resolved correctly later.

Other Terms

(PLM-41897), BOM3, Ballooning, MBOM

Reason and Prerequisites

During balloon derivation, paths are evaluated across multiple assembly levels.

When processing the top-level assembly, components originating from subassemblies are temporarily evaluated as potential candidates.
If these paths cannot be resolved at top level, warnings are generated.

The warning handling does not distinguish between intermediate evaluation results and final resolution results.
As a consequence, warnings remain although the positions are correctly determined when the subassembly is processed.

Solution

The ballooning logic was corrected to remove obsolete warnings once the corresponding balloon position is successfully resolved.

Warnings created during intermediate evaluation steps are now linked to the corresponding input row and removed from the error table if the position is found in a later processing step.

Changed function module: /DSCSAG/PLM_MBOM_BALLOONING
