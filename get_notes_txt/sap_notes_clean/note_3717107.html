Symptom

Supplier has the invoicing submission method of External XML Invoice, the XML has a date but when the invoice arrives at SAP Business Network, the date is different.

Reproducing the Issue

  1. Send the invoice by submission method as External XML Invoice with the desired date.
  2. Business Network receives the invoice but in the cXML the date is different and it is not the one in the XML.

Cause

This happens because the date recorded in the XML does not have a defined timezone. 

The SAP Business Network requires a valid timestamp value in ISO 8601 format for all documents posted by suppliers. By default, the SAP Business Network uses the system date and time zone of the host (Pacific Standard Time) for the timestamp value. If the selected date is not the current date, the timestamp will be set to noon of that day. 

For example, if the XML has this information in the invoice date: <DataWytworzeniaFa>2026-02-10T16:02:55.314556431</DataWytworzeniaFa>

The sequences provided end in fractions of a second and do not include either a "Z" or an offset like "+00:00". This means they are interpreted as "unknown time zone" or assumed to be local. 

That format, despite being in ISO 8601, does not have a defined timezone so even though day 10 is shown in the XML, Business Network may interpret it as day 11. 

In the ISO 8601 format, a time zone can be indicated in two ways: 

  1. The letter "Z" at the end of the date and time, indicating that the time is in UTC (Coordinated Universal Time).
  2. A time offset (known as UTC offset), such as "+02:00" or "-05:00", specifying the difference from local time to UTC.

Resolution

To resolve this, the date in the XML must have the specific and desired timezone; if this specification does not exist, it may happen that the XML shows the 10th, for example, and in SAP Business Network it appears as the 11th.
