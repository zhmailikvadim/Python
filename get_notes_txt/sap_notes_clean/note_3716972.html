Symptom

In disassembly-related cost calculations, fixed labor costs defined in activity types are calculated incorrectly when the routing contains more than one operation or activity.
If the work center formula for fixed duration includes the number of employees, the system does not consistently consider this formula during planning calculations. As a result, fixed costs and time quantities are calculated incorrectly in planning scenarios such as MRP calculation, long-term planning, and disassembly orders. The incorrect plan costs are subsequently transferred into follow-on objects.

Other Terms

IS-MFM, MEAT, Disassembly costing, fixed costs, plan cost calculation, SAP Meat and Fish Management by msg.

Reason and Prerequisites

The issue occurs when:
An activity type is defined with fixed costs only.
The routing operation contains a fixed duration that is independent of production quantity.
The number of employees is maintained in the routing operation.
The work center uses a formula that multiplies fixed duration by the number of employees.
When the disassembly calculation includes multiple operations or activities, the system loses the activity parameters during planning calculations. As a result, the work center formula for fixed duration is ignored or only partially evaluated, leading to incorrect time and cost calculation

Solution

History:
This SAP Note was created on 17.02.2026.

Description of Correction:
The system logic for disassembly-related cost and time calculation has been corrected to ensure that work center formulas are consistently evaluated in all planning scenarios.
As a result, fixed labor costs are now calculated correctly and consistently in plan costing, MRP calculation, long-term planning, and disassembly orders.

Functional Dependencies:
None.

Manual Changes:
No manual activities are required after applying this correction.
