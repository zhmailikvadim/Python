Symptom

A high frequency of calls triggering a failing HTTP allowlist check causes DB locks on the corresponding logging tables HTTP_LOG_LIST, HTTP_MLOG_LIST, HTTP_CORS_LOG or HTTP_CORS_MLOG.

Reason and Prerequisites

Many failing allowlist check within a short period of time try to log the call and therefore access the same DB table at the same point in time, resulting in DB locks on the respective table.

Solution

With the implementation of this SAP Note, identical failing checks are only logged once every 10 minutes. As a potential DB lock would most likely be caused by a high frequency of identical calls (especially Cross-origin resource sharing (CORS) requests) in a realistic scenario, this is ought to prevent all DB locks in practice.
