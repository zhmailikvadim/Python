Symptom

You have received notification email(s) informing you about the change in the Certification Authority (CA) accepted by your web services user.

Find a sample below about the certificate we are speaking about on this article, it can be found in your SAP Cloud Integration for data services (CI-DS) tenant > Administration > Users > WebServicesUser.

The purpose of this article is to clarify the actions required on customer side in relation to this change.

Image/data in this KBA is from SAP internal systems, sample data, or demo systems. Any resemblance to real data is purely coincidental. 

Environment

SAP Cloud Integration for data services

Cause

Following an industry-wide initiative led by Google, major certificate authorities (CAs) will stop issuing client certificates.

The initial plan was for year 2026 and recently, some CA changed/postponed the plan for year 2027.

On SAP IBP side, DigiCert was the CA issuing the Client Default certificate generated in your SAP IBP. Until now, the Client Default certificate was issued by "DigiCert Global G2 TLS RSA SHA256 2020 CA1".

Going forward, DigiCert will stop issuing certificates for use in client authentication with this issuer certificate.

As a consequence, SAP has decided to introduce a new issuing authority for client certificates: SAP Cloud Root CA. 

From March 2026 onwards, newly issued certificates will be signed by this CA rather than DigiCert.

Resolution

In your SAP IBP tenant, you need to switch the communication users for outbound connection from the old Client Default certificate to the new one.

The client default certificate of IBP and signed by SAP Cloud Root CA can be downloaded from the Outbound user of the communication system used for SAP_COM_0035 and uploaded  inside CI-DS > Administration > Users > WebServicesUser > "Upload Certificate".

For more details, see:

  • Configuring the Outbound Communication with SAP Cloud Integration for Data Services | SAP Help Portal
