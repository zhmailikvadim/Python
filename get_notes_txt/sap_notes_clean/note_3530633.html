Symptom

This announcement contains information about the legal change Digital monitoring of goods movement (Ψηφιακή παρακολούθηση διακίνησης αγαθών), published on August 6, 2024, by the Independent Authority of Public Revenue (IAPR):

  • Α.1122/06-08-2024 (sets the phases, timeline & deadlines, liable entities, and the timing of issuance and transmission to myDATA)
  • Α.1123/06-08-2024 (sets the content and format of e-delivery documents, the method and the process of issuance and transmission to myDATA, the transmission channels, the roles of related entities, and further details)

This requirement is meant to impact both domestic and international transportation.

IAPR provides for two implementations phases A and B, with the planned effective dates being as follows:

- Companies with gross income above 200K euros (in the year 2022), and companies that trade medicines and medical consumables, energy products (fuels), building materials, olives, and olive oil, irrespective of their gross income, are expected to comply with phase A from December 1, 2024, and with phase B from April 1, 2025.

- All other companies (except the public sector, municipalities, and a few other exceptions) are expected to comply with phases A & B from April 1, 2025.

This information is based on legislation and regulations which could be subject to change at any time by the respective authorities. The information will be updated if required.​​​

  • Phase A
    • Issuance of documents related to the shipment of goods bearing a QR code that denotes their successful submission to myDATA​. These documents include delivery notes, combined invoice–delivery notes, and internal stock transfers (plant to plant).
    • Transmission of goods movement related information to myDATA for delivery notes, revenue invoices, internal stock transfers, and notification of the recipient.
    • Ability to control the transportation of goods by scanning the QR code of the respective accompanying documents.
  • Phase B
    • Digital monitoring of complicated transportation (loads and post-loads of goods) during the entire movement of goods.
    • Receipt of goods and transaction completion by scanning the transportation document's QR code.
    • Quantitative and qualitative control of goods by the recipient, with issuance and submission to myDATA of quantity receipt documents in case of discrepancies (surpluses - deficits).
    • Transmission of goods movement related information to myDATA for expense invoices (purchases from abroad, title-deeds, and so on).
    • Inclusion of information regarding correlated entities (carrier, sold-to, and so on), and associated documents in the XML.
    • Use of the Combined Nomenclature (CN) as per EU 2658/87 regulation for material codes in the XML.

Solution

SAP intends to provide a feature package or patch to solve the symptom that is described in this SAP Note. The section References below lists the related feature package or patch once they become available. Be aware that References can only be confirmed at feature package or patch release date. SAP delivers feature packages or patches only for selected releases at its own discretion, based on the business impact and the complexity of implementation.

The following implementation is planned to fulfill this legal requirement:

  • Electronic protocol for e-Books to be enhanced to support Deliveries, Goods Return and Stock Transactions, Goods Receipt PO, Returns, Goods Issue and Stock Transfer.
  • New spp file to be delivered.

Planned delivery date for legal change solution: 28 Feb 2025.
