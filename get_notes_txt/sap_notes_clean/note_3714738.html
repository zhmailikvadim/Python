Symptom

When there is a split RGDIR (due to IT, PSS, or other absenteeism), the total reported base does not reach the monthly maximum, even though the employee’s gross salary clearly exceeds it. The excess from one period cannot be redistributed to the unused capacity of the other period, resulting in a loss of contributions for the employee and possible incoherence between SLD and CRA validations. These differences might not be detected nor warned by the SLD Bases file response, resulting in false positive scenarios.

Environment

  • Human Capital Management (HCM)
  • SAP R/3
  • SAP R/3 Enterprise 4.7
  • SAP ERP Core Component
  • SAP ERP
  • SAP enhancement package for SAP ERP
  • SAP enhancement package for SAP ERP, version for SAP HANA 

Reproducing the Issue

Example 1

When a payroll is split (e.g., employee works days 1-15, then goes on sick leave days 16-30), each period applies the daily maximum cap independently. This could happen when an employee has a salary increment and during the same transition period, has an absence that would contribute based on previous months. Here the employee would have 4,500 € regularly as a salary during previous months, but during this month, starts to earn 6,000 € monthly.

PeriodGross BaseProportional MaxReported Base
Days 1-15 (work)3,000 €2,454.75 €2,454.75 €
Days 16-30 (sick leave)2,250 €2,454.75 €2,250 €

Total reported: 4,704.75 €
Expected monthly max: 4,909.50 €
Gap: 204.75 € lost

The excess from period 1 (545.25 €) cannot offset the unused capacity in period 2 (204.75 €). Each period is capped independently, resulting in lower total contributions than if calculated as a single monthly period.

Example 2

When a payroll is split (e.g., employee works days 1-28, then has a different situation days 29-30 with PSS), each period applies the daily maximum cap independently. This applies to all employees contributing over the maximum based on the proportional earnings [(base salary / contribution days × worked days) > monthly maximum], in scenarios with PSS and payroll splits.

PeriodGross BaseProportional MaxReported Base
Days 1-28 (work)5,600 €4,582.20 €4,582.20 €
Days 29-30 (PSS)92.08 €327.30 €92.08 €

Total reported (current): 4,674.28 €
Expected monthly max: 4,909.50 €
Gap: 235.22 € lost

The excess from period 1 (1,017.80 €) cannot offset the unused capacity in period 2 (235.22 €). Each period is capped independently, resulting in a reported base of 4,674.28 € instead of 4,909.50 €, which creates an inconsistency with the solidarity base (782.58 €) that is correctly calculated using the full monthly maximum.

Cause

The root cause in all these cases is a limitation of the HCM Spain payroll solution that uses the international payroll framework with a "hard" RGDIR split. When the payroll period is divided (due to IT, PSS, or other absences), each split applies the maximum contribution cap independently, without any visibility into what will be calculated in the next interval. This means the system cannot redistribute the excess from one period to cover the unused capacity in another, resulting in a total reported base lower than the monthly maximum, even when the employee’s gross salary clearly exceeds it.

This creates inconsistencies with calculations that do consider the full month, such as the additional solidarity base (Contribución Adicional de Solidaridad / additional solidarity base).

Resolution

For the analysis of these cases a tool is provided in SAP note 2839798 - CALC: Log for calculation of Social Security bases intervals with a new processing node on the payroll schema:

The ESLOG log offers an interval view (ABSENCE, WORKED) similar to the SLD, useful for comparing and validating DBMAX results:

  • The ABSENCE intervals should include the sum of SLD concepts for the absence.
  • The WORKED intervals are related to the intervals of concept 500 in SLD.

It confirms the lack of a 1:1 equivalence between SLD absenteeism intervals and bases returned by DBMAX: SLD trims by benefit/maximums, while DBMAX may return the total base for the absence period. The new log allows for breaking down and analyzing these differences.

For all these split scenarios, you must define manual contribution bases for the worked period to reach the monthly cap via SAP Note 2447355 - CALC: Manual contribution base 

Currently, DBMAX should only be used for comparison and analysis of full-time employees with monthly contributions, without VNDs or any partiality. This KBA will be updated to reflect the adherence to new scenarios and features.
