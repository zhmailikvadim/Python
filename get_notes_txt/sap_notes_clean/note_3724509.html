Symptom

  • During an automatic payment run (F110) for multiple company codes using the same payment method (e.g., foreign payments), the intermediary bank field REGUH-BNKS1 is not populated for one company code, while it is populated for another.
  • The same vendor is used for both company codes.
  • Bank chain maintenance (FIBPU) exists, and the FBZP setup appears identical for the involved company codes (both acting as paying company codes).

Environment

  • SAP R/3
  • SAP ERP
  • SAP ERP Central Component
  • SAP enhancement package for SAP ERP
  • SAP enhancement package for SAP ERP, version for SAP HANA
  • SAP S/4HANA

Reproducing the Issue

  1. Execute transaction F110.
  2. Enter parameters for multiple company codes and the relevant payment method.
  3. Create the payment proposal.
  4. Review the proposal log or inspect table REGUH for the proposals/payments.
  5. Observe that REGUH-BNKS1 is blank for one company code but filled for the other.

Cause

  • Population of REGUH-BNKS1 (intermediary bank) relies on bank chain determination (transaction FIBPU).
  • Bank chain determination requires an exact match of key data, including:
    • Payee (vendor) for which the payment is being made.
    • Payer’s bank key and country used by the open item/payment (house bank of the paying company code).
    • Recipient bank data (country, bank key, and account/IBAN).
  • If there is no matching FIBPU entry for a specific payer bank key/country and recipient bank data, no bank chain was determined, and REGUH-BNKS1 remains empty.
  • Identical FBZP configuration across company codes does not ensure REGUH-BNKS1 is filled; the result depends on the presence of a matching FIBPU entry for the actual payer bank key/country and the exact recipient bank details.

Resolution

  • Align and complete bank chain maintenance (FIBPU) for all payer bank keys/countries used by the involved company codes:
    1. In FIBPU, create/maintain a bank chain entry for the vendor with the specific payer’s bank key and country used by the open item/payment in the affected company code.
    2. Use the same recipient bank data (country, bank key, and account/IBAN) as maintained for the working case, ensuring an exact match (including IBAN formatting).
    3. If multiple payer house banks can be selected by the payment program, maintain corresponding FIBPU entries for each relevant payer bank key/country combination.

  •  After updating FIBPU:
    1. Rebuild the payment proposal in F110.
    2. Verify in the proposal log or REGUH that REGUH-BNKS1 is now populated for the affected company code.

  •  Additional checks:
    1. Confirm the open items are posted/selected with the intended payer house bank so that the correct FIBPU entry can be matched.
    2. Ensure any changes are transported consistently across systems, if applicable.
