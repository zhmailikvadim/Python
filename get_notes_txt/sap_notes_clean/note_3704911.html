Symptom

In the process of clearing down payments, several inconsistencies have been identified with respect to the DRC report VAT Control Statement for Czechia. These issues have been raised through customer incidents and relate to specific steps in the down payment clearing workflow. To prevent incorrect reporting, it is recommended to follow the process outlined below.

Other Terms

VAT Control Statement, VAT ledger, Czechia, Down payment, SAP S/4HANA Cloud, public edition

Reason and Prerequisites

The scenarios described in this note concern specific aspects of the down payment clearing process that are not fully documented in the current SAP Help Portal. Certain transactions may be posted in ways that lead to incorrect or incomplete reporting in the Czech VAT Control Statement.

Solution


The following scenarios were identified within the down payment clearing process and indicate the need to follow the recommended steps to ensure correct reporting in the VAT Control Statement for Czechia.

1. SD Invoice Clearing

Final closing invoices should be cleared within the SD module initiating closing invoice in billing plan. In case of clearing final invoice in other applications, such as 'Clear Incoming Payments', it will not be correctly reported in VAT Control Statement for Czechia.

2. Supplier Down Payment Correction

When a correction for a supplier down payment (down payment return) is processed, the House Bank information must always be provided.

3. Tax Code Mismatch

The scenario with different tax codes in a down payment and an invoice within the same month is not supported.

4. Partial Clearing or Overpayments

The CZ VAT Control Statement supports only the 'Post on Account' method within the applications 'Clear Outgoing Payments' or 'Clear Incoming Payments' when dealing with partial clearing or overpayments.
