Symptom

As described in Payroll Deductions Formulas - 122nd Edition Effective January 1, 2026, in Yukon Basic Personal Amount (BPAYT) Formula, "The Yukon Basic Personal Amount (BPAYT) formula mirrors the federal Basic Personal Amount (BPAF) formula." this SAP Note provides technical changes to optimize BPAYT calculation.

Other Terms

T511K

Reason and Prerequisites

Reason

For Year-end 2025, Yukon constants were updated with federal amounts to produce the correct output. Technical changes are now introduced to retrieve the amounts directly from the federal constants.

Prerequisites

The following Support Package level is the minimum prerequisite:

S4HCM 102 HR SP 00
S4HCM 101 HR SP 04
S4HCM 100 HR SP 17
SAP_HR 6.08 HR SP C8

For more information, access the SAP ERP Human Capital Management - Support Package Schedules (SAP_HR) or SAP S/4HANA On Premise Human Capital Management (S4HCM).

When you install an SAP Note, some prerequisites might be required. The prerequisites are SAP Notes that have already been released and that change the same context of the current one. Then, even if these prerequisites are not necessarily related to the symptom of this SAP Note, you must implement them due to technical reasons.

Solution

From now on, the system uses the same constants and calculation method for Yukon Basic Personal Amount (BPAYT) that the Federal Basic Personal Amount (BPAF) uses.

The Yukon constants are delimited to 2025.

You can find these constants in the Payroll Parameters (V_T511K) view.

Yukon obsolete constants from 2026 onward:

  • BMAYT - Max BPA 
  • BMIYT - Min BPA
  • NMAYT - Max Net Income
  • NMIYT - Min Net Income

Federal constants used for Yukon calculation from 2026 onward:

  • FBMAX - Max BPA
  • FBMIN - Min BPA
  • NIMAX - Max Net Income
  • NIMIN - Min Net Income
