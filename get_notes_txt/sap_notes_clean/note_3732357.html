Symptom

In the calendar year of the first-in-life contract of employment, the employee is entitled to 1/12 part of the yearly vacation limit after each worked month. Until now it was assumed that for such employees the yearly vacation limit was always 20 days.

Nevertheless, it may occur that the employee has passed the date of 10 years’ seniority (that is relevant for the yearly vacation limit calculation), but he/she is still entitled to the so-called first-year leave.

This scenario happens for example when the seniority period involves higher education and/or some activity periods relevant from year 2026 (see SAP Note 3666533).

It is important that for the monthly entitlement calculation of the first-year leave, 1/12 part of 26 days (instead of 20) must be considered from the anniversary date of the 10 years’ seniority.

Other Terms

IT2006, first-year leave, seniority, 3666533.

Reason and Prerequisites

Gap in the implementation.

Solution

The quota generation process has been changed.

Apply the correction by importing the Support Package specified for your release, or by using the Note Assistant.
