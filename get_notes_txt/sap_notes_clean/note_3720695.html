Symptom

In the View Transport Data application, TKM values are not equal to the product of Weight (ton) multiplied by Distance (km). 

Environment

Sustainability Footprint Management

Reproducing the Issue

  1. Open the View Transport Data application.
  2. Select the Measures: TKM, Weight (kg), and Distance (km).
  3. Select the Dimensions: Product, Mode of Transport, Parcel Source Location, Parcel Target Location, and Leg Key.

Cause

The calculation of TKM does not follow the assumption that TKM equals Weight (ton) multiplied by Distance (km). Instead, TKM is calculated on a parcel/leg/weight/distance level, requiring the inclusion of parcel numbers in the extraction.

Resolution

Refer to the following example for clarification:

ParcelLegDistance (km)Weight (kg)TKM
1a_b20500(20 * 500) / 1000 = 10
1b_c30500(30 * 500) / 1000 = 15
1c_d100500(100 * 500) / 1000 = 50
2f_e100050(1000 * 50) / 1000 = 50
3g_h9005(900 * 5) / 1000 = 4.5

Sum the TKM column to verify the correct value (e.g., 129.5).

Avoid summing the distance and multiplying it by the weight without considering parcel numbers, as this leads to incorrect values.
