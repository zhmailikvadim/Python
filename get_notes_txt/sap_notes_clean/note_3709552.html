Symptom

  • The invoice is generated at the sales side on the last day of period X. Similarly, data processing occurs on the last day at the RAR side as well.
  • However, any invoice correction postings (IC) in the FARR_D_POSTING table are created in the subsequent period, X+1.
  • This only happens in Optimized Contract Management (OCM).

Environment

  • SAP S/4HANA
  • SAP S/4HANA Finance

Reproducing the Issue

  1. Generate an invoice at the sales side on the last day of the current period.
  2. Post the invoice on the same day.
  3. Process the associated data in RAR on this same date.
  4. Note that the invoice correction (IC) posting in FARR_D_POSTING table is created to the following period instead of being recorded in the period when the invoice was created in sales side.

Cause

In OCM (Optimized Contract Management), it is not possible to process invoice RAIs into a date before the FARR_D_POB_CTYPE-EFFECTIVE_DATE. System is working as designed.

Resolution

When a POB Finalization occurs, the FARR_D_POB_CTYPE-EFFECTIVE_DATE is set to FARR_D_POB_CTYPE-FINAL_DATE + 1 as per SAP Note 2899715 "Change of determination of Effective date". By default, POB finalization is considered a Prospective change. A POB finalization (cancellation) means that if this prospective contract change occurs on the last day of a period, the forward adjustment of the Effective Date results in invoice correction (IC) entries falling into the incorrect period when invoice RAIs are processed after this change. In OCM, processing invoice RAIs to a date before the Effective Date/prospective split date is not possible. For technical reasons, a reconciliation key is always requested by the system after the prospective split date.

Every date before the FARR_D_POB_CTYPE-EFFECTIVE_DATE field is "locked" when a Prospective change is made, ensuring that invoice RAIs are processed according to the latest FARR_D_POB_CTYPE-EFFECTIVE_DATE if it is greater than the POSTING_DATE. If the FARR_D_POB_CTYPE-EFFECTIVE_DATE is less than the FARR_D_INVOICE-POSTING_DATE, then the FARR_D_INVOICE-POSTING_DATE is used to determine the period into which the invoice correction (IC) entries fall in FARR_D_POSTING table, considering the period has Status = ‘Open’, seen in the FARR_C_CLOSE table.
