Symptom

During hiring of an Indian employee in Onboarding, for Compliance Forms → EPF (Employee Provident Fund) → Form 26 (Joint Declaration):

  • The field “For enrolment – Choose” is mandatory
  • Only two options are available:
    1. For those members who join EPF for the first time
    2. For those members who join employment again after superannuation

For employees who:

  • Already have an existing EPF membership (UAN available)
  • Are not retired/superannuated

There is no appropriate option, causing confusion during onboarding.

Image/data in this KBA is from SAP internal systems, sample data, or demo systems. Any resemblance to real data is purely coincidental.

Environment

SAP SuccessFactors Onboarding

Cause

Expected Behavior. The EPF Joint Declaration (Form 26) in Onboarding is a standard SAP-delivered compliance form based on the statutory EPFO format. As per this design, the enrollment field is mandatory and only provides two predefined options. The scenario of an existing EPF member (with UAN) joining a new employer is not explicitly represented as a separate option in the form.

Resolution

As confirmed by SAP Product Management, the form follows the official EPFO declaration format, and the available options should be interpreted accordingly. For employees who already have an EPF membership (UAN exists) and are not retired, the recommended approach might be to select: “For those members who join employment again after superannuation.”

This guidance is based on SAP’s current design of the form. If required, customers should further validate this selection with their internal compliance team or the Employees’ Provident Fund Organisation (EPFO) to ensure alignment with statutory requirements.
