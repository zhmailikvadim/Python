Symptom

This article provides guidance on the correct SAP configuration for Brazilian withholding taxes INSS (Social Security) and IRPF (Individual Income Tax), including dependencies, simplified discounts, and other specific legal requirements.

Environment

  • SAP ERP
  • SAP S/4HANA
  • SAP S/4HANA Cloud Private Edition
  • Brazil Withholding Taxes 

Resolution

1. INSS and IRRF Base Calculation

By law, the INSS withholding tax amount must always be deducted from the calculation base of the IRRF withholding tax.

  • Automatic Calculation: To ensure this dependency is calculated automatically by the system, both INSS and IRRF taxes must be configured to be calculated at the time of invoice posting.
  • Manual Adjustment: If your process requires IRRF to be calculated at the time of payment posting, the system will not automatically deduct the INSS. You must manually edit the IRRF base amount to subtract the INSS value.

2. Simplified Discount (MP 1.171/2023)

The optional simplified discount is only supported by the standard SAP solution when taxes are calculated at invoice posting.

  • Implementation: For instructions on how to implement this, refer to SAP Note 3379646 and the attached Configuration Guide, which explains how to set up the withholding tax types.
  • Limitation: The standard solution does not support the simplified discount at payment posting.

3. Workaround for Simplified Discount at Payment

While SAP does not provide a standard localized solution for the simplified discount at payment, you can achieve this by manually setting a base amount exemption.

  1. Navigate to the following SPRO path:
    • Financial Accounting > Financial Accounting Global Settings > Withholding Tax > Extended Withholding Tax > Calculation > Minimum and Maximum Amounts > Define Min/Max Amounts for Withholding Tax Codes (or Types, depending on your scenario)
  2. In the "WHT base amt exempt." field, enter the value of the simplified discount (e.g., 607,20 as of 2026). This amount will be deducted from the withholding tax base during payment posting.

4. General Configuration

You must assign the withholding tax types to the relevant Company Code and define the G/L accounts for posting.

  • Assign to Company Code:
    • SPRO: Financial Accounting > Financial Accounting Global Settings > Withholding Tax > Extended Withholding Tax > Company Code > Assign Withholding Tax Types to Company Codes (V_T001WT)
  • Define G/L Accounts:
    • SPRO: Financial Accounting > Financial Accounting Global Settings > Withholding Tax > Extended Withholding Tax > Posting > Accounts for Withholding Tax > Define Accounts for Withholding Tax to be Paid Over

5. Other Important Considerations

    • IRRF Daily Accumulation: IRRF should not be configured for Daily Accumulation. SAP does not provide localization for this scenario. For more details, see SAP Note 2461392.
    • Reduced Base (MP 582/2012): For information on configuring withholding tax with a reduced base, refer to KBA 1904221.
