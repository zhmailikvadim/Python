Symptom

  • Granting SELECT privilege at the schema level provides access to all tables within the schema, including future tables.
  • Attempting to restrict access to specific tables by executing REVOKE SELECT at the table level does not override the schema-level SELECT privilege.
  • Users retain SELECT access to tables even after REVOKE SELECT is executed at the table level.

Environment

SAP HANA Security & User Management

Cause

  • Schema-level privileges in SAP HANA are additive and cannot be selectively negated by object-level REVOKE commands when the role itself was granted the broader privilege.
  • SAP HANA does not support a "deny/exception" mechanism in its privilege model to restrict access to specific tables while granting schema-level privileges.

Resolution

  1. Understand that schema-level privileges in SAP HANA are additive and apply to all existing and future objects within the schema.
  2. Note that object-level REVOKE commands cannot override schema-level GRANT privileges.
  3. Refer to SAP KBA 3631829  for additional information on schema privileges and their behavior.
  4. Consider alternative approaches to implement granular data protection controls, such as avoiding schema-level privileges and granting table-level privileges individually.
